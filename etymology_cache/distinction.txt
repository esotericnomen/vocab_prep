c.1200, one of the parts into which something is divided; mid-14c. as action of distinguishing, from
Old French distinction and directly from Latin distinctionem (nominative distinctio) separation,
distinction, discrimination, noun of action from past participle stem of distinguere (see <a
href=/index.php?term=distinguish&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>distinguish</a>).
Meaning distinctive nature or character is late 14c. Meaning excellence or eminence (what
distinguishes from others) is first recorded 1690s.
