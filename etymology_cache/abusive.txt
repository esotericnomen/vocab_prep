1530s (implied in abusively), originally improper, from Middle French abusif, from Latin abusivus,
from abus-, past participle stem of abuti (see <a href=/index.php?term=abuse&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>abuse</a> (v.)). Meaning full of abuse is from 1580s. Abuseful was used 17c.,
and Shakespeare has abusious (Taming of the Shrew, 1594). Related: Abusiveness.
