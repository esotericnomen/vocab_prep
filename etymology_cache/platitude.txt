1812, dullness, from French platitude flatness, vapidness (late 17c.), from Old French plat flat
(see <a href=/index.php?term=plateau&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>plateau</a> (n.));
formed on analogy of latitude, etc. Meaning a flat, dull, or commonplace remark is recorded from
1815. Related: Platitudinous. Hence platitudinarian (n.), 1855; platitudinize (1867).
