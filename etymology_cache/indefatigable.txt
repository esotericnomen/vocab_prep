1580s (implied in indefatigably), from French indefatigable (15c.), from Latin indefatigabilis that
cannot be wearied, from in- not (see <a href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>in-</a> (1)) + defatigare to tire out, from de- utterly, down, away + fatigare
to weary (see <a href=/index.php?term=fatigue&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>fatigue</a>).
