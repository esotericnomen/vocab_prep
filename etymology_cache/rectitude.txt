early 15c., quality of being straight, from Middle French rectitude (14c.), from Late Latin
rectitudinem (nominative rectitudo) straightness, uprightness, from Latin rectus straight (see <a
href=/index.php?term=right&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>right</a> (adj.1)). Sense of
upright in conduct or character is from 1530s.
