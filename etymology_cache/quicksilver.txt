Old English cwicseolfor, literally living silver, translating Latin argentum vivum (source also of
Italian argento vivo), literally living silver; so called from its liquid mobility. See <a
href=/index.php?term=quick&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>quick</a> (adj.) + <a
href=/index.php?term=silver&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>silver</a> (n.). Similar
formation in Dutch kwikzilver, Old High German quecsilbar, German quecksilber, French vif-argent,
Italian argenta viva.
