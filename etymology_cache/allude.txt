1530s, mock, from Middle French alluder or directly from Latin alludere to play, sport, joke, jest,
from ad- to (see <a href=/index.php?term=ad-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ad-</a>) +
ludere to play (see <a href=/index.php?term=ludicrous&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>ludicrous</a>). Meaning make an indirect reference, point in passing is from
1570s. Related: Alluded; alluding.
