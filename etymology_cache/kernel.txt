Old English cyrnel seed, kernel, pip, from Proto-Germanic *kurnilo- (cognates: Middle High German
kornel, Middle Dutch cornel), from the root of corn seed, grain (see <a
href=/index.php?term=corn&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>corn</a> (n.1)) + -el,
diminutive suffix. Figurative sense of core or central part of anything is from 1550s.
