c.1600, tendency, of habits, pursuits, etc.; 1640s as state of being self-addicted, from Latin
addictionem (nominative addictio) an awarding, a devoting, noun of action from past participle stem
of addicere (see <a href=/index.php?term=addict&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>addict</a> (v.)). Earliest sense was less severe: inclination, penchant, but
this has become obsolete. In main modern sense it is first attested 1906, in reference to opium
(there is an isolated instance from 1779, with reference to tobacco).
