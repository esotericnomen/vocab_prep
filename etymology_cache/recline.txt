early 15c., from Old French recliner rest, lay; bend, lean over (13c.) and directly from Latin
reclinare to bend back, to lean back; cause to lean, from re- back, against (see <a
href=/index.php?term=re-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>re-</a>) + clinare to bend,
from PIE *klei-n-, suffixed form of *klei to lean (see <a
href=/index.php?term=lean&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>lean</a> (v.)). Related:
Reclined; reclining.  <blockquote> Recline is always as strong as lean, and generally stronger,
indicating a more completely recumbent position, and approaching lie. [Century
Dictionary]</blockquote>
