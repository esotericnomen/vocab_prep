early 14c., from Old French controver (Modern French controuver) to find out, contrive, imagine,
from Late Latin contropare to compare (via a figure of speech), from Latin com- with (see <a
href=/index.php?term=com-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>com-</a>) + tropus song,
musical mode, from Greek tropos figure of speech (see <a
href=/index.php?term=trope&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>trope</a>).  <br /><br />
Sense evolution (in French) was from invent with ingenuity to invent falsely. Spelled contreve until
unexplained 15c. sound change that also affected briar, friar, choir. Related: Contrived;
contriving.
