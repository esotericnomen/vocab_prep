mid-14c., loftiness of thought or purpose, from Old French magnanimit&#233; high-mindedness,
generosity of spirit, from Latin magnanimitatem (nominative magnanimitas) greatness of soul, high-
mindedness, from magnanimus having a great soul, from magnus great (see <a
href=/index.php?term=magnate&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>magnate</a>) + animus mind,
soul, spirit (see <a href=/index.php?term=animus&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>animus</a>). Probably a loan-translation of Greek megalopsykhos high-souled,
generous (Aristotle) or megathymus great-hearted.
