1560s, of the French middle class, from French bourgeois, from Old French burgeis, borjois town
dweller (see <a href=/index.php?term=bourgeoisie&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>bourgeoisie</a>). Sense of socially or aesthetically conventional is from 1764;
in communist and socialist writing, as a noun, a capitalist (1883).<blockquote>It is better to be a
good ordinary bourgeois than a bad ordinary bohemian. [Aldous Huxley, 1930]</blockquote>
