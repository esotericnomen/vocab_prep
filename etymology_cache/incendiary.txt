c.1400 as a noun, person who sets malicious fires; mid-15c. as an adjective, capable of being used
to set fires, from Latin incendiarius causing a fire, from incendium conflagration, from incendere
set on fire, figuratively, incite, rouse, enrage, from in- into, in, on, upon (see <a
href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (2)) + *candere to set
alight, cause to glow, related to candere to shine (see <a
href=/index.php?term=candle&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>candle</a>). Figurative
sense of enflaming passions (adj.) is from 1610s. Military use, of bombs, shells, etc., attested
from 1871. The obsolete verb incend is attested from c.1500.
