1530s, from Middle French atrocit&eacute; or directly from Latin atrocitatem (nominative atrocitas)
cruelty, fierceness, harshness, noun of quality from atrox fierce, cruel, frightful, from PIE *atro-
ek-, from root *ater- fire (see <a href=/index.php?term=atrium&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>atrium</a>) + *okw- see (see <a href=/index.php?term=eye&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>eye</a> (n.)); thus of fiery or threatening appearance. The meaning an
atrocious deed is from 1793.
