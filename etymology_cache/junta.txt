1620s, Spanish legislative council, from Spanish and Portuguese junta council, meeting, convention,
from Medieval Latin iuncta joint, from Latin iuncta, fem. past participle of iungere to join (see <a
href=/index.php?term=jugular&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>jugular</a>).  <br /><br />
Meaning political or military group in power first recorded 1640s as junto (from confusion with
Spanish nouns ending in -o), originally with reference to the Cabinet Council of Charles I. Modern
spelling in this sense is from 1714; popularized 1808 in connection with councils formed across
Spain to resist Napoleon.
