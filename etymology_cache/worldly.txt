Old English woruldlic earthly, secular, from the roots of <a
href=/index.php?term=world&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>world</a> and <a
href=/index.php?term=like&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>like</a> (adj.). A common
Germanic compound (Old Frisian wraldlik, Old Saxon weroldlik, Middle Dutch wereldlik, German
weltlich, Old Norse veraldligr). Worldly-wise is recorded from c.1400.
