1590s, a wandering, straying, from Latin aberrationem (nominative aberratio) a wandering, noun of
action from past participle stem of aberrare to wander out of the way, lose the way, go astray, from
ab- away (see <a href=/index.php?term=ab-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ab-</a>) +
errare to wander (see <a href=/index.php?term=err&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>err</a>). Meaning deviation from the normal type first attested 1846.
