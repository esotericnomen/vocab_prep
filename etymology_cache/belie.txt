Old English beleogan to deceive by lies, from <a href=/index.php?term=be-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>be-</a> + <a href=/index.php?term=lie&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>lie</a> (v.1) to lie, tell lies. Current sense of to contradict as a lie is
first recorded 1640s. The other verb lie once also had a formation like this, from Old English
belicgan, which meant to encompass, beleaguer, and in Middle English was a euphemism for to have sex
with (i.e. to lie with carnally).
