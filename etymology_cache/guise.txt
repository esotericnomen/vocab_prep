late 13c., style or fashion of attire, from Old French guise manner, fashion, way, from Frankish
*wisa or some similar Germanic source (cognate with Old High German wisa manner, wise; see <a
href=/index.php?term=wise&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>wise</a> (n.)). Sense of
assumed appearance is from 1660s, from earlier meaning mask, disguise (c.1500).
