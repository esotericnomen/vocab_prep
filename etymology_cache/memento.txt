c.1400, Psalm cxxxi in the Canon of the Mass (which begins with the Latin word Memento and in which
the dead are commemorated), from Latin memento remember, imperative of meminisse to remember,
recollect, think of, bear in mind, a reduplicated form, related to mens mind (see <a
href=/index.php?term=mind&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>mind</a> (n.)). Meaning
reminder, object serving as a warning is from 1580s; sense of keepsake is first recorded 1768.
