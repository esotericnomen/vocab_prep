mental view or survey, 1742, from <a href=/index.php?term=out&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>out</a> (adv.) + <a href=/index.php?term=look&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>look</a> (v.). The meaning prospect for the future is attested from 1851.
Earliest sense was a look-out (1660s). The literal sense of vigilant watch, act or practice of
looking out (1815) is rare; look-out being used instead for this.
