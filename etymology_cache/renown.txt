c.1300, from Anglo-French renoun, Old French renon renown, fame, reputation, from renomer make
famous, from re- repeatedly (see <a href=/index.php?term=re-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>re-</a>) + nomer to name, from Latin nominare to name (see <a
href=/index.php?term=nominate&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>nominate</a>). The Middle
English verb reknouen make known, acknowledge has been assimilated to the noun via <a
href=/index.php?term=renowned&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>renowned</a>. In old
German university slang, a reknowner (German renommist) was a boaster, a swaggerer.
