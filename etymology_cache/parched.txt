1650s, dry, parched, from French aride (15c.) or directly from Latin aridus dry, arid, parched, from
arere to be dry, from PIE root *as- to burn, glow (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ash&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ash</a> (n.1)). Figurative
sense of uninteresting is from 1827. Related: Aridly.
