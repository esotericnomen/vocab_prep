1737; earlier as un-Latinized theocraty (1620s), form of government in which God is recognized as
supreme ruler and his laws form the statute book, originally of the sacerdotal government of Israel
before the rise of kings, from later Greek theokratia (Josephus), literally  the rule of God, from
theos god (see <a href=/index.php?term=theo-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>theo-</a>)
+ kratos a rule, regime, strength (see <a href=/index.php?term=-cracy&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>-cracy</a>). Meaning priestly or religious body wielding political and civil
power is recorded from 1825.  Related: Theocratic (1741).
