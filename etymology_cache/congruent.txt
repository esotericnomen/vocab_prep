early 15c., suitable, proper, from Latin congruentem (nominative congruens) agreeing, fit, suitable,
present participle of congruere, literally to come together, agree, correspond with, from com- with
(see <a href=/index.php?term=com-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>com-</a>) + a lost
verb *gruere, *ruere fall, rush, perhaps from PIE *ghrei- to rub, grind (see <a
href=/index.php?term=chrism&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>chrism</a>). Geometry sense
attested by 1706.
