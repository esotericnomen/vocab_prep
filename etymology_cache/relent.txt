late 14c., to melt, soften, dissolve, from <a href=/index.php?term=re-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>re-</a> + Latin lentus slow, viscous, supple (see <a
href=/index.php?term=lithe&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>lithe</a>), perhaps on model
of Old French rallentir. Sense of become less harsh or cruel first recorded 1520s. The notion
probably is of a hard heart melting with pity. Related: Relented; relenting.
