1760 gloomy, shadowy (earlier sombrous, c.1730), from French sombre dark, gloomy, from Old French
sombre (14c.), from an adjective from Late Latin subumbrare to shadow, from sub under (see <a
href=/index.php?term=sub-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>sub-</a>) + umbra shade,
shadow, perhaps from a suffixed form of PIE *andho- blind, dark (see <a
href=/index.php?term=umbrage&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>umbrage</a>). Related:
Somberly; somberness.
