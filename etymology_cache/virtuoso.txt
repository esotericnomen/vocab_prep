1610s, scholar, connoisseur, from Italian virtuoso (plural virtuosi), noun use of adjective meaning
skilled, learned, of exceptional worth, from Late Latin virtuosus (see <a
href=/index.php?term=virtuous&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>virtuous</a>). Meaning
person with great skill, one who is a master of the mechanical part of a fine art (as in music) is
first attested 1743.
