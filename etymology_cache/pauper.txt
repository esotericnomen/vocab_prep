1510s, person destitute of property or means of livelihood, from Latin pauper poor, not wealthy, of
small means (see <a href=/index.php?term=poor&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>poor</a>
(adj.)). Originally in English a legal word, from Latin phrase in forma pauperis (late 15c.) in the
character of a poor person, thus allowed to sue in court without legal fees.
