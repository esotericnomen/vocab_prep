c.1400, a fold, a crease, from Anglo-French pleit, Old French ploit, earlier pleit, fold, manner of
folding, from Latin plicatus, past participle of plicare to lay, fold, twist (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ply&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ply</a> (v.1)). Meaning
interlaced strands of hair, ribbon, etc. is from 1520s, perhaps from <a
href=/index.php?term=plait&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>plait</a> (v.).
