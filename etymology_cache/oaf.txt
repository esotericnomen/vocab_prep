1620s, auf, oph (modern form from 1630s), a changeling; a foolish child left by the fairies
[Johnson], from a Scandinavian source such as Norwegian alfr silly person, in Old Norse elf (see <a
href=/index.php?term=elf&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>elf</a>). Hence, a misbegotten,
deformed idiot. Until recently, some dictionaries still gave the plural as oaves.
