mid-15c., from Middle French comparatif, from Latin comparativus pertaining to comparison, from
comparat-, past participle stem of comparare (see <a
href=/index.php?term=comparison&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>comparison</a>).
Originally grammatical; general sense is from c.1600; meaning involving different branches of a
subject is from 1670s. Related: Comparatively.
