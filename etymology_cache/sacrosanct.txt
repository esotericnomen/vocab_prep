superlatively sacred or inviolable, c.1600, from Latin sacrosanctus protected by religious sanction,
consecrated with religious ceremonies, from sacro, ablative of sacrum religious sanction (from
neuter singular of sacer sacred) + sanctus, past participle of sancire make sacred (for both, see <a
href=/index.php?term=sacred&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>sacred</a>). Earlier in
partially anglicized form sacro-seint (c.1500).
