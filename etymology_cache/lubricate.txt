1620s, to make slippery or smooth (especially by the application of an oil), from Latin lubricatus,
past participle of lubricare to make slippery or smooth, from lubricus slippery (see <a
href=/index.php?term=lubricant&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>lubricant</a> (adj.)).
Related: Lubricated; lubricating. Earlier verb was lubrify (1610s), from Medieval Latin lubrificare.
