late 14c., schoolmaster, teacher, from Old French pedagoge teacher of children (14c.), from Latin
paedagogus, from Greek paidagogos slave who escorts boys to school and generally supervises them,
later a teacher, from pais (genitive paidos) child (see <a
href=/index.php?term=pedo-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>pedo-</a>) + agogos leader,
from agein to lead (see <a href=/index.php?term=act&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>act</a> (n.)). Hostile implications in the word are at least from the time of
Pepys (1650s).  Related: Pedagogal.
