1570s, from Latin belligerantem (nominative belligerans), past participle of belligerare to wage
war, from bellum war (see <a href=/index.php?term=bellicose&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>bellicose</a>) + gerere to bear, to carry (see <a
href=/index.php?term=gest&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>gest</a>). The noun meaning
party or nation at war is from 1811. Related: Belligerently.
