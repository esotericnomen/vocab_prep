1540s, from Latin rostrum, name of the platform stand for public speakers in the Forum in ancient
Rome. It was decorated with the beaks of ships taken in the first naval victory of the Roman
republic, over Antium, in 338 B.C.E., and the word's older sense is end of a ship's prow, literally
beak, muzzle, snout, originally means of gnawing, instrument noun form of rodere to gnaw (see <a
href=/index.php?term=rodent&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>rodent</a>). Compare
claustrum lock, bar, from claudere to shut.  Extended sense of any platform for public speaking is
first recorded 1766. Classical plural form is rostra.
