remonstrate (v.)

1590s, make plain, back-formation from remonstration, or else from Medieval Latin remonstratus, past
participle of  remonstrare to demonstrate (see remonstrance). Meaning to exhibit or present strong
reasons against is from 1690s. Related: Remonstrated; remonstrating.


expostulate (v.)

1530s, to demand, to claim, from Latin expostulatus, past participle of expostulare to demand
urgently, remonstrate, from ex- from (see ex-) + postulare to demand (see postulate (v.)).
Friendlier sense is first recorded in English 1570s. Related: Expostulated; expostulating.
