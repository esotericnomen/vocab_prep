1650s, originally in the military sense of a regrouping for renewed action after a repulse, from <a
href=/index.php?term=rally&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>rally</a> (v.1). Sense of
mass meeting to stir enthusiasm first attested 1840, American English. Sense of gathering of
automobile enthusiasts is from 1932, from French rallye, itself from the English noun. Sports sense
of long series of hits in tennis, etc., is from 1881, earlier series of back-and-forth blows in a
boxing match (1829).
