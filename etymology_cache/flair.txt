mid-14c., an odor, from Old French flair odor or scent, from flairer to smell, from Vulgar Latin
*flagrare, dissimilated from Latin fragrare emit (a sweet) odor (see <a
href=/index.php?term=fragrant&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>fragrant</a>). Sense of
special aptitude is American English, 1925, perhaps from notion of a hound's ability to track scent.
