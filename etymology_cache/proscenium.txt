c.1600, stage of an ancient theater, from Latin proscaenium, from Greek proskenion the space in
front of the scenery, also entrance of a tent, from pro in front (see <a
href=/index.php?term=pro-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>pro-</a>) + skene stage, tent,
booth (see <a href=/index.php?term=scene&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>scene</a>).
Modern sense of space between the curtain and the orchestra is attested from 1807.
