late 14c., art of judging characters from facial features, from Old French phizonomie and directly
from Late Latin physiognomia, from Greek physiognomia the judging of a person's nature by his
features, from physio- (see <a href=/index.php?term=physio-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>physio-</a>) + gnomon (genitive gnomonos) judge, indicator (see <a
href=/index.php?term=gnomon&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>gnomon</a>). Meaning face,
countenance, features is from c.1400. Related: Physiognomical.
