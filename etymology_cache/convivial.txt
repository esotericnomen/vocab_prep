1660s, pertaining to a feast, from Late Latin convivialis, from Latin convivium a feast, from
convivere to carouse together, from com- together (see <a
href=/index.php?term=com-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>com-</a>) + vivere to live
(see <a href=/index.php?term=vital&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>vital</a>). Meaning
sociable is from 18c. Related: Conviviality.
