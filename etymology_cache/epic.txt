1580s, perhaps via Middle French &eacute;pique or directly from Latin epicus, from Greek epikos,
from epos word, story, poem, from PIE *wekw- to speak (see <a
href=/index.php?term=voice&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>voice</a>). Extended sense of
grand, heroic first recorded in English 1731. From 1706 as a noun in reference to an epic poem, A
long narrative told on a grand scale of time and place, featuring a larger-than-life protagonist and
heroic actions [Miller Williams, Patterns of Poetry].
