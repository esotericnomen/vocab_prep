late 14c., obsessed with plundering, extremely greedy, from Old French ravinos, of people,
rapacious, violent, of water, swift-flowing, from raviner to seize, from ravine violent rush,
robbery (see <a href=/index.php?term=ravine&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ravine</a>).
Meaning voracious, very hungry is from early 15c. Related: Ravenously; ravenousness.
