1590s, immodest, wanton, saucy, from Middle French petulant (mid-14c.), from Latin petulantem
(nominative petulans) wanton, froward, saucy, insolent, present participle of petere to attack,
assail; strive after; ask for, beg, beseech (see <a
href=/index.php?term=petition&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>petition</a> (n.)).
Meaning peevish, irritable first recorded 1775, probably by influence of <a
href=/index.php?term=pet&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>pet</a> (n.2). Related:
Petulantly.
