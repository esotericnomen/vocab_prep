1590s, from Latin rabies madness, rage, fury, related to rabere be mad, rave (see <a
href=/index.php?term=rage&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>rage</a> (v.)). Sense of
extremely fatal infectious disease causing madness in dogs was a secondary meaning in Latin. Known
<a href=/index.php?term=hydrophobia&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>hydrophobia</a> in
humans.
