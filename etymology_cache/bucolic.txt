1610s, earlier bucolical (1520s), from Latin bucolicus, from Greek boukolikos pastoral, rustic, from
boukolos cowherd, herdsman, from bous cow (see <a href=/index.php?term=cow&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>cow</a> (n.)) + -kolos tending, related to Latin colere to till (the ground),
cultivate, dwell, inhabit (the root of <a href=/index.php?term=colony&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>colony</a>). Middle Irish b&#250;achaill, Welsh bugail shepherd are Celtic
words form from the same root material as Greek boukolos.
