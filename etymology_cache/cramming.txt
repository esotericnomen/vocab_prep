cram (v.)

Old English crammian press something into something else, from Proto-Germanic *kram-/*krem-
(cognates: Old High German krimman to press, pinch, Old Norse kremja to squeeze, pinch), from PIE
root *ger- to gather (cognates: Sanskrit gramah heap, troop, Old Church Slavonic gramota heap, Latin
gremium bosom, lap). Meaning study intensely for an exam originally was British student slang first
recorded 1803. Related: Crammed; cramming.
