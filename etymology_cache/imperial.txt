late 14c., having a commanding quality, from Old French imperial (12c.), from Latin imperialis of
the empire or emperor, from imperium (see <a href=/index.php?term=empire&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>empire</a>). Meaning pertaining to an empire (especially the Roman) is from
late 14c. Imperial presidency in a U.S. context traces to Arthur Schlesinger Jr.'s book on the Nixon
administration (1974). Related: Imperially.
