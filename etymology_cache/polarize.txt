1811, in optics, from French polariser, coined by French physicist &Eacute;tienne-Louis Malus
(1775-1812) as a term in optics, from Modern Latin polaris polar (see <a
href=/index.php?term=polar&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>polar</a>). Transferred sense
of to accentuate a division in a group or system is first recorded 1949 in Arthur Koestler. Related:
Polarized; polarizing.
