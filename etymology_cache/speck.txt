Old English specca small spot, stain, of unknown origin; probably related to Dutch speckel speck,
speckle, Middle Dutch spekelen to sprinkle (compare <a
href=/index.php?term=speckle&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>speckle</a> (v.)). Meaning
tiny bit developed c.1400. As a verb, 1570s, from the noun. Related: Specked.
