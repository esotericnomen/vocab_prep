c.1400, physical, human, mortal, from Old French carnal and directly from Medieval Latin carnalis
natural, of the same blood, from Latin carnis of the flesh, genitive of caro flesh, meat (see <a
href=/index.php?term=carnage&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>carnage</a>). Meaning
sensual is from early 15c.; that of worldly, sinful is from mid-15c. Carnal knowledge is attested
from early 15c. and was in legal use by 1680s.
