mid-15c., to take as spouse, marry, from Old French espouser marry, take in marriage, join in
marriage (11c., Modern French &eacute;pouser), from Latin sponsare, past participle of spondere make
an offering, perform a rite, hence to engage oneself by ritual act (see <a
href=/index.php?term=spondee&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>spondee</a>).  <br /><br />
Extended sense of adopt, embrace a cause, party, etc., is from 1620s. Related: Espoused; espouses;
espousing. For initial e-, see <a href=/index.php?term=e-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>e-</a>.
