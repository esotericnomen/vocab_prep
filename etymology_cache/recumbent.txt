1705, from Latin recumbentem (nominative recumbens), present participle of recumbere recline, lie
down, lie down again; of things, to fall, sink down, settle down, from re- back (see <a
href=/index.php?term=re-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>re-</a>) + -cumbere to lie down
(see <a href=/index.php?term=succumb&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>succumb</a>).
Related: Recumbency (1640s). A verb, recumb, has been attempted in English occasionally since 1670s.
