c.1600, inconstant, from Latin versatilis turning, revolving, moving, capable of turning with ease
to varied subjects or tasks, from past participle stem of versare keep turning, be engaged in
something, turn over in the mind, frequentative of vertere to turn (see <a
href=/index.php?term=versus&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>versus</a>). Meaning able to
do many things well is from 1762 in English.
