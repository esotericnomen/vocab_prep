late 14c., due proportion of elements or qualities, from <a
href=/index.php?term=temper&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>temper</a> (v.). The sense
of characteristic state of mind, inclination, disposition is first recorded 1590s; that of calm
state of mind, tranquility in c.1600; and that of angry state of mind (for bad temper) in 1828.
Meaning degree of hardness and resiliency in steel is from late 15c.
