1768, from French expatrier banish (14c.), from ex- out of (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ex-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ex-</a>) + patrie native land,
from Latin patria one's native country, from pater (genitive patris) father (see <a
href=/index.php?term=father&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>father</a> (n.); also
compare <a href=/index.php?term=patriot&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>patriot</a>).
Related: Expatriated; expatriating. The noun is from 1818, one who has been banished; main modern
sense of one who chooses to live abroad is 1902.
