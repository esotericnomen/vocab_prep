c.1600, from Latin castigatus, past participle of castigare to correct, set right; purify; chastise,
punish, from castus pure (see <a href=/index.php?term=caste&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>caste</a>) + agere to do (see <a
href=/index.php?term=act&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>act</a> (n.)). The notion
behind the word is make someone pure by correcting or reproving him.<blockquote>If thou didst put
this soure cold habit on To castigate thy pride, 'twere well. [Shakespeare, Timon IV.iii
(1607)]</blockquote>  Related: Castigated; castigating; castigator; castigatory.
