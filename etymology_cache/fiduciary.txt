1630s, from Latin fiduciarius (holding) in trust, from fiducia trust from root of fidere to trust
(see <a href=/index.php?term=faith&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>faith</a>). In Roman
law, fiducia was a right transferred in trust; paper currency sense (1878) is because its value
depends on the trust of the public. As a noun, from 1630s.
