escrow (n.)

1590s, from Anglo-French escrowe, from Old French escroe scrap, small piece, rag, tatter, single
parchment, from a Germanic source akin to Old High German scrot a scrap, shred, a piece cut off (see
shred (n.)). Originally a deed delivered to a third person until a future condition is satisfied,
which led to sense of deposit held in trust or security (1888).
