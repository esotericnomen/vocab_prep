bawdy (adj.)

late 14c., soiled, dirty, filthy, from bawd + -y (2). Meaning lewd is from 1510s, from notion of
pertaining to or befitting a bawd; usually of language (originally to talk bawdy).
<blockquote>Bawdy Basket, the twenty-third rank of canters, who carry pins, tape, ballads and
obscene books to sell. [Grose, Dictionary of the Vulgar Tongue, 1785]</blockquote>  Related:
Bawdily; bawdiness.
