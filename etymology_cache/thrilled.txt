early 14c., to pierce, penetrate, metathesis of Old English &thorn;yrlian to perforate, pierce, from
&thorn;yrel hole (in Middle English, also nostril), from &thorn;urh through (compare Middle High
German d&uuml;rchel pierced, perforated; see <a href=/index.php?term=through&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>through</a>) + -el. Meaning give a shivering, exciting feeling is first
recorded 1590s, via metaphoric notion of pierce with emotion. Related: Thrilled; thrilling.
