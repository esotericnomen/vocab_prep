1650s, pertaining to a feast, from Latin festivus festive, joyous, gay, from festum festival,
holiday, noun use of neuter of adjective festus (see <a
href=/index.php?term=feast&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>feast</a> (n.)).  <br /><br
/>  Meaning mirthful is attested by 1774. Unattested from 1651 to 1735; modern use may be a back-
formation from festivity. Related: Festively; festiveness.
