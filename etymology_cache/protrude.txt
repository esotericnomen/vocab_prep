1610s, to thrust forward or onward, to drive along; 1640s, to cause to stick out, from Latin
protrudere thrust forward; push out, from pro- forward (see <a
href=/index.php?term=pro-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>pro-</a>) + trudere to thrust
(see <a href=/index.php?term=extrusion&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>extrusion</a>).
Intransitive meaning jut out, bulge forth recorded from 1620s. Related: Protruded; protruding.
