late 15c., from Old French sumptueux or directly from Latin sumptuosus costly, very expensive;
lavish, wasteful, from sumptus, past participle of sumere to borrow, buy, spend, eat, drink,
consume, employ, take, take up, contraction of *sub-emere, from sub- under (see <a
href=/index.php?term=sub-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>sub-</a>) + emere to take, buy
(see <a href=/index.php?term=exempt&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>exempt</a> (adj.)).
Related: Sumptuously; sumptuousness.
