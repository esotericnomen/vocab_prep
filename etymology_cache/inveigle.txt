late 15c., to blind (someone's) judgment, alteration of Middle French aveugler delude, make blind,
from Vulgar Latin *aboculus without sight, blind, from Latin ab- without (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ab-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ab-</a>) + oculus eye (see <a
href=/index.php?term=eye&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>eye</a> (n.)). Loan-translation
of Greek ap ommaton without eyes. Meaning to win over by deceit, seduce is 1530s.
