1660s (adv.), 1764 (adj.), from French impromptu (1650s), from Latin in promptu in readiness, from
assimilated form of in- into, in, on, upon (see <a href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>in-</a> (2)) + promptu, ablative of promptus readiness, from past participle of
promere to bring out, from pro- before, forward, for + emere to obtain (see <a
href=/index.php?term=exempt&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>exempt</a>).
