1650s, from Latin fortuitus happening by chance, casual, accidental, from forte by chance, ablative
of fors chance (related to fortuna; see <a href=/index.php?term=fortune&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>fortune</a>). It means accidental, undesigned not fortunate. Earlier in this
sense was fortuit (late 14c.), from French. Related: Fortuitously; fortuitousness.
