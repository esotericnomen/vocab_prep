1743, raised platform around an ancient arena, also projecting base of a pedestal, from Latin podium
raised platform, from Greek podion foot of a vase, diminutive of pous (genitive podos) foot (see <a
href=/index.php?term=foot&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>foot</a> (n.)). Meaning raised
platform at the front of a hall or stage is from 1947.
