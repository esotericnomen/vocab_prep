1610s, from Late Latin soliloquium a talking to oneself, from Latin solus alone (see <a
href=/index.php?term=sole&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>sole</a> (adj.)) + loqui speak
(see <a href=/index.php?term=locution&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>locution</a>).
Also used in translation of Latin Liber Soliloquiorum, a treatise by Augustine, who is said to have
coined the word, on analogy of Greek monologia (see <a
href=/index.php?term=monologue&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>monologue</a>). Related:
Soliloquent.
