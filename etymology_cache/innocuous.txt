innocuous (adj.)

1590s, from Latin innocuus harmless, from in- not (see in- (1)) + nocuus hurtful, from root of
nocere to injure, harm, from *nok-s-, suffixed form of PIE root *nek- (1) death (see necro-).
Related: Innocuously; innocuousness.


nocuous (adj.)

1630s, noxious, harmful, from Latin nocuus harmful, from stem of nocere to hurt, injure, harm (see
innocuous).


nociceptive (adj.)

1904, from Latin noci-, comb. form of nocere to do harm (see innocuous) + second element from
receptive.
