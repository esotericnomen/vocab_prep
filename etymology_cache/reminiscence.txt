1580s, act of remembering, from Middle French reminiscence (14c.) and directly from Late Latin
reminiscentia remembrance, recollection (a loan-translation of Greek anamnesis), from Latin
reminiscentem (nominative reminiscens), present participle of reminisci remember, recall to mind,
from re- again (see <a href=/index.php?term=re-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>re-</a>)
+ minisci to remember, from root of mens mind, from PIE root *men- mind, understanding, reason (see
<a href=/index.php?term=mind&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>mind</a> (n.)). Meaning a
recollection of something past is attested from 1811.
