1650s, formed in English from Latin equester (genitive equestris) of a horseman, from eques
horseman, knight, from equus horse (see <a href=/index.php?term=equine&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>equine</a>). As a noun, one who rides on horseback, from 1791. The pseudo-
French fem. equestrienne is attested from 1848.
