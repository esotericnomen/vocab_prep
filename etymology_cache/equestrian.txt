equestrian (adj.)

1650s, formed in English from Latin equester (genitive equestris) of a horseman, from eques
horseman, knight, from equus horse (see equine). As a noun, one who rides on horseback, from 1791.
The pseudo-French fem. equestrienne is attested from 1848.


blackleg (n.)

swindler, especially in equestrian events, 1771, from black (adj.) + leg (n.), but the exact
signification is uncertain.


tack (n.2)

horse's harness, etc., 1924, shortening of tackle (n.) in sense of equipment. Tack in a non-
equestrian sense as a shortening of tackle is recorded in dialect from 1777.
