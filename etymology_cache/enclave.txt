1868, from French enclave, from Old French enclaver enclose, comprise, include (13c.), from Late
Latin inclavare shut in, lock up, from Latin in- in (see <a
href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (2)) + clavis key (see
<a href=/index.php?term=slot&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>slot</a> (n.2)). Enclaved
surrounded by land owned by another is attested in English from mid-15c., from Middle French
enclaver.
