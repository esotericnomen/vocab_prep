disaffected (adj.)

estranged, hostile, usually in reference to authority, 1630s, past participle adjective from
disaffect. Related: Disaffectedly; disaffectedness.


affected (adj.)

past participle adjective from affect (v.2); 1530s in the now-obsolete sense favorably disposed
(preserved in disaffected); meaning artificially displayed is recorded from 1580s.


disaffect (v.)

1610s, from dis- + affect (v.1). Related: Disaffected; disaffecting.
