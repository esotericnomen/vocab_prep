late 14c., from Old French flus flowing, rolling, bleeding, or directly from Latin fluxus flowing,
loose, slack, past participle of fluere to flow (see <a
href=/index.php?term=fluent&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>fluent</a>). Originally
excessive flow (of blood or excrement); an early name for dysentery; sense of continuous succession
of changes is first recorded 1620s. The verb is early 15c., from the noun.
