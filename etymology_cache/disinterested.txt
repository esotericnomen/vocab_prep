1610s, unconcerned, the sense we now would ascribe to <a

the sense of impartial going to disinteressed (c.1600). See <a
href=/index.php?term=dis-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>dis-</a> + <a
href=/index.php?term=interest&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>interest</a>. Modern sense
of disinterested is first attested 1650s. As things now stand, disinterested means free from
personal bias, while uninterested means caring nothing for the matter in question. Related:
Disinterestedly; disinterestedness.
