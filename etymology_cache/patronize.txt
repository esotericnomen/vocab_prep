1580s, to act as a patron towards, from <a href=/index.php?term=patron&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>patron</a> + <a href=/index.php?term=-ize&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>-ize</a>, or from Old French patroniser. Meaning treat in a condescending way
is first attested 1797; sense of give regular business to is from 1801. Related: Patronized; <a
