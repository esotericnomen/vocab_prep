1610s, from Latin sonorus resounding, from sonor sound, noise, from sonare to sound (see <a
href=/index.php?term=sonata&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>sonata</a>). Related:
Sonorously; sonorousness. Earlier was sonouse (c.1500), from Medieval Latin sonosus; sonourse having
a pleasing voice (c.1400), from sonor + -y (2).
