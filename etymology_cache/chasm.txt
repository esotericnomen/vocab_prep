1590s, deep crack in the earth, from Latin chasma, from Greek khasma yawning hollow, gulf, related
to khaskein to yawn, and thus to <a href=/index.php?term=chaos&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>chaos</a>. In English in 17c. often spelled chasma. Figurative use from 1640s.
Related: Chasmal; chasmic.
