1650s, study of the soul, from Modern Latin psychologia, probably coined mid-16c. in Germany by
Melanchthon from Latinized form of Greek psykhe- breath, spirit, soul (see <a
href=/index.php?term=psyche&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>psyche</a>) + logia study of
(see <a href=/index.php?term=-logy&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>-logy</a>). Meaning
study of the mind first recorded 1748, from Christian Wolff's Psychologia empirica (1732); main
modern behavioral sense is from early 1890s.
