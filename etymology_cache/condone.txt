1857, from Latin condonare to give up, remit, permit, from com-, intensive prefix (see <a
href=/index.php?term=com-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>com-</a>), + donare to give
(see <a href=/index.php?term=donation&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>donation</a>).
Originally a legal term in the Matrimonial Causes Act, which made divorce a civil matter in Britain.
Related: Condoned; condoning.
