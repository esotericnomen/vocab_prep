1580s, apostate, probably (with change of suffix) from Spanish renegado, originally Christian turned
Muslim, from Medieval Latin renegatus, noun use of past participle of renegare deny (see <a
href=/index.php?term=renege&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>renege</a>). General sense
of turncoat is from 1660s. The form renegate, directly from Medieval Latin, is attested in English
from late 14c. As an adjective from 1705.
