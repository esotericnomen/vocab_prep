recession (n.)

1640s, act of receding, a going back, from French r&#233;cession a going backward, a withdrawing,
and directly from Latin  recessionem (nominative recessio) a going back, noun of action from past
participle stem of recedere (see recede).    Sense of temporary decline in economic activity, 1929,
noun of action from recess (q.v.): <blockquote>The material prosperity of the United States is too
firmly based, in our opinion, for a revival in industrial activity -- even if  we have to face an
immediate recession of some magnitude -- to be long delayed. [Economist, Nov. 2,
1929]</blockquote>Ayto notes, There was more than a hint of euphemism in the coining of this term.
