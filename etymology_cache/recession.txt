1640s, act of receding, a going back, from French r&#233;cession a going backward, a withdrawing,
and directly from Latin  recessionem (nominative recessio) a going back, noun of action from past
participle stem of recedere (see <a href=/index.php?term=recede&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>recede</a>).  <br /><br />  Sense of temporary decline in economic activity,
1929, noun of action from <a href=/index.php?term=recess&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>recess</a> (q.v.): <blockquote>The material prosperity of the United States is
too firmly based, in our opinion, for a revival in industrial activity -- even if  we have to face
an immediate recession of some magnitude -- to be long delayed. [Economist, Nov. 2,
1929]</blockquote>Ayto notes, There was more than a hint of euphemism in the coining of this term.
