1540s, a medical word for excess of body fluid, from Late Latin plethora, from Greek plethore
fullness, from plethein be full (see <a href=/index.php?term=pleio-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>pleio-</a>). Figurative meaning too-muchness, overfullness in any respect is
first recorded 1700. Related: Plethoric.
