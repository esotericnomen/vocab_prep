c.1300, gastlich, from gast (adj.), past participle of gasten to frighten, from Old English
g&aelig;stan to torment, frighten (see <a href=/index.php?term=ghost&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>ghost</a>) + -lich -ly. Spelling with gh- developed 16c. from confusion with
ghost. As an adverb, from 1580s. Related: Ghastliness.
