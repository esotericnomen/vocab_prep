early 15c., repudiation, from Late Latin repulsionem (nominative repulsio) a repelling, noun of
action from past participle stem of repellere (see <a
href=/index.php?term=repel&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>repel</a>). Meaning action of
forcing or driving back is attested from 1540s. Sense of strong dislike is from 1751.
