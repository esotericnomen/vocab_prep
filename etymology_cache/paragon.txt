1540s, from Middle French paragon a model, pattern of excellence (15c., Modern French parangon),
from Italian paragone, originally touchstone to test gold (early 14c.), from paragonare to test on a
touchstone, compare, from Greek parakonan to sharpen, whet, from para- on the side (see <a
href=/index.php?term=para-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>para-</a> (1)) + akone
whetstone, from PIE root *ak- sharp, pointed (see <a
href=/index.php?term=acrid&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>acrid</a>).
