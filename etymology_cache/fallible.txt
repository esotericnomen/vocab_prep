fallible (adj.)

early 15c., from Medieval Latin fallibilis liable to err, deceitful. literally that can be deceived,
from Latin fallere deceive (see fail).


fallibility (n.)

1630s; see fallible + -ity.


infallible (adj.)

early 15c., from Medieval Latin infallibilis, from in- not, opposite of (see in- (1)) + Late Latin
fallibilis (see fallible). In reference to Popes, attested from 1870. Related: Infallibly.
