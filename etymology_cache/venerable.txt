early 15c., worthy of respect, from Old French venerable and directly from Latin venerabilis worthy
of reverence or respect, from venerari to worship, revere (see <a
href=/index.php?term=veneration&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>veneration</a>). As a
title, used in reference to ecclesiastics (in the Anglican church, specifically of archdeacons) or
those who had obtained the first degree of canonization. Related: Venerably; venerability.
