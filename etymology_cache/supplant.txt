early 14c., to trip up, overthrow, defeat, dispossess, from Old French suplanter, sosplanter to trip
up, overthrow, drive out, usurp, or directly from Latin supplantare trip up, overthrow, from sub
under (see <a href=/index.php?term=sub-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>sub-</a>) +
planta sole of the foot (see <a href=/index.php?term=plant&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>plant</a> (n.)). Meaning replace one thing with another first recorded 1670s.
There is a sense evolution parallel in Hebrew akabh he beguiled, from akebh heel.
