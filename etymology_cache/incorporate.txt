late 14c., to put (something) into the body or substance of (something else), from Late Latin
incorporatus, past participle of incorporare unite into one body, from Latin in- into, in, on, upon
(see <a href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (2)) + corpus
(genitive corporis) body (see <a href=/index.php?term=corporeal&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>corporeal</a>). Meaning to legally form a body politic is from 1460s. Related:
Incorporated; incorporating.
