1916, extravert (spelled with -o- after 1918, by influence of introvert), from German Extravert,
from extra outside (see <a href=/index.php?term=extra-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>extra-</a>) + Latin vertere to turn (see <a
href=/index.php?term=versus&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>versus</a>). With <a
href=/index.php?term=introvert&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>introvert</a>, words that
had been used in English by doctors and scientists in various literal senses since 1600s, but
popularized in a psychological sense by Carl Jung. Related: Extroverted.
