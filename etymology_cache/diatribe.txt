1640s (in Latin form in English from 1580s), discourse, critical dissertation, from French diatribe
(15c.), from Latin diatriba learned discussion, from Greek diatribe employment, study, in Plato,
discourse, literally a wearing away (of time), from dia- away (see <a
href=/index.php?term=dia-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>dia-</a>) + tribein to wear,
rub, from PIE root *tere- (1) to rub, turn, twist (see <a
href=/index.php?term=throw&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>throw</a> (v.)). Sense of
invective is 1804, apparently from French.
