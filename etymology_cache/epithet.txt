1570s, descriptive name for a person or thing, from Middle French &eacute;pith&egrave;te or directly
from Latin epitheton, from Greek epitheton something added, adjective often used as noun, from
neuter of epithetos attributed, added, from epitithenai to add on, from epi in addition (see <a
href=/index.php?term=epi-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>epi-</a>) + tithenai to put
(see <a href=/index.php?term=theme&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>theme</a>).
