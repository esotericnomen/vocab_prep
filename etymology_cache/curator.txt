mid-14c., from Latin curator overseer, manager, guardian, agent noun from curatus, past participle
of curare (see <a href=/index.php?term=cure&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>cure</a>
(v.)). Originally of those put in charge of minors, lunatics, etc.; meaning officer in charge of a
museum, library, etc. is from 1660s.
