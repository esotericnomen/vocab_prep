1540s, to disown, disinherit (children), from Latin abdicatus, past participle of abdicare to
disown, disavow, reject (specifically abdicare magistratu renounce office), from ab- away (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ab-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ab-</a>) + dicare proclaim,
from stem of dicere to speak, to say (see <a href=/index.php?term=diction&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>diction</a>). Meaning divest oneself of office first recorded 1610s. Related:
Abdicated; abdicating.
