1530s, from Latin assiduus attending; continually present, incessant; busy; constant, from assidere
to sit down to, thus constantly occupied at one's work; from ad to (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ad-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ad-</a>) + sedere to sit (see
<a href=/index.php?term=sedentary&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>sedentary</a>). The
word acquired a taint of servility in 18c. Related: Assiduously; assiduousness.
