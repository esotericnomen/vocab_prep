late 13c., prouesse, from Old French proece prowess, courage, brave deed (Modern French prouesse),
from prou, later variant of prud brave, valiant, from Vulgar Latin *prodem (source also of Spanish
proeza, Italian prodezza; see <a href=/index.php?term=proud&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>proud</a>). Prow was in Middle English as a noun meaning advantage, profit,
also as a related adjective (valiant, brave), but it has become obsolete. In 15-17th c. often a
monosyllable [OED].
