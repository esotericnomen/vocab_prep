1650s, an eye-witnessing, from Modern Latin autopsia, from Greek autopsia a seeing with one's own
eyes, from autos- self (see <a href=/index.php?term=auto-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>auto-</a>) + opsis a sight (see <a
href=/index.php?term=eye&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>eye</a> (n.)). Sense of
dissection of a body to determine cause of death is first recorded 1670s, probably from the same
sense in French autopsie (1570s).
