squeamish (adj.)

late 14c., variant (with -ish) of squoymous disdainful, fastidious (early 14c.), from Anglo-French
escoymous, which is of unknown origin. Related: Squeamishly; squeamishness.  <blockquote>  He was
somdel squaymous  Of fartyng, and of speche daungerous  [Chaucer, Miller's Tale, c.1386]
</blockquote>
