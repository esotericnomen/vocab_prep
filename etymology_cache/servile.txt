late 14c., from Latin servilis of a slave (as in Servile Wars, name given to the slave revolts in
the late Roman Republic), also slavish, servile, from servus slave (see <a
href=/index.php?term=serve&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>serve</a> (v.)). Earliest
sense was legal, servile work being forbidden on the Sabbath; sense of cringing, fawning first
recorded c.1600.
