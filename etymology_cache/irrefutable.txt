irrefutable (adj.)

1610s, from Latin irrefutabilis irrefutable, from assimilated form of in- not, opposite of (see in-
(1)) + refutabilis, from refutare (see refute). Related: Irrefutably; irrefutability.


uncontrollable (adj.)

1570s, irrefutable, from un- (1) not + controllable. From 1590s as not subject to authority; meaning
that cannot be restrained is from 1640s. Related: Uncontrollably.
