rolling (adj.)

14c., past participle adjective from roll (v.). Of prairie land from 1819. From mid-15c. as a verbal
noun. Rolling pin is recorded from late 15c. Rolling paper for cigarettes, etc., is from 1969.
Rolling stock wheeled vehicles on a railroad (locomotives, carriages, etc.) is from 1853.
<blockquote>The rollyng stone neuer gatherth mosse. [John Heywood, A dialogue conteinying the nomber
in effect of all the proverbes in the Englishe tongue, 1546]</blockquote>
