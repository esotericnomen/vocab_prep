1570s, originally of medicines, that tends to prevent disease, from Middle French prophylactique
(16c.) and directly as a Latinized borrowing of Greek prophylaktikos precautionary, from
prophylassein keep guard before, ward off, be on one's guard, from pro- before (see <a
href=/index.php?term=pro-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>pro-</a>) + phylassein, Ionic
variant of phylattein to watch over, to guard, but also cherish, keep, remain in, preserve (see <a
href=/index.php?term=phylactery&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>phylactery</a>).  <br
/><br />  The noun is first recorded 1640s, a medicine or treatment to prevent disease; meaning
condom is from 1943, replacing earlier preventive (1822), preventative (1901). Condoms originally
were used more to thwart contagious disease than to prevent pregnancy.
