early 15c., from Latin assimilatus feigned, pretended, fictitious, past participle of assimilare to
make like, from ad- to (see <a href=/index.php?term=ad-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>ad-</a>) + simulare make similar, from similis like, resembling (see <a
href=/index.php?term=similar&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>similar</a>). Originally
transitive (with to); intransitive use first recorded 1837. Related: Assimilated; assimilating.
