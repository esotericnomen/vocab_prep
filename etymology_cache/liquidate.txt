1570s, to reduce to order, to set out clearly (of accounts), from Late Latin or Medieval Latin
liquidatus, past participle of liquidare to melt, make liquid or clear, clarify, from Latin liquidus
(see <a href=/index.php?term=liquid&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>liquid</a>). Sense
of clear away (a debt) first recorded 1755. The meaning wipe out, kill is from 1924, possibly from
Russian likvidirovat. Related: Liquidated; liquidating.
