c.1300, nygromauncy, divination by communication with the dead, from Old French nigromancie magic,
necromancy, witchcraft, sorcery, from Medieval Latin nigromantia (13c.), from Latin necromantia
divination from an exhumed corpse, from Greek nekromanteia, from nekros dead body (see <a
href=/index.php?term=necro-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>necro-</a>) + manteia
divination, oracle, from manteuesthai to prophesy, from mantis prophet (see <a
href=/index.php?term=mania&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>mania</a>). Spelling
influenced in Medieval Latin by niger black, on notion of black arts. Modern spelling is a mid-16c.
correction. Related: Necromantic.
