one who practices affected attitudes, 1866, from French poseur, from verb poser affect an attitude
or pose, from Old French poser to put, place, set (see <a
href=/index.php?term=pose&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>pose</a> (v.1)). The word is
