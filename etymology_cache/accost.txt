1570s, from Middle French accoster move up to, from Italian accostare or directly from Late Latin
accostare come up to the side, from Latin ad- to (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ad-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ad-</a>) + costa rib, side (see
<a href=/index.php?term=coast&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>coast</a> (n.)). The
original notion is of fleets of warships attacking an enemy's coast. Related: Accosted; accosting.
