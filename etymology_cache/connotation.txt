1530s, from Medieval Latin connotationem (nominative connotatio), from connotat-, past participle
stem of connotare signify in addition to the main meaning, a term in logic, literally to mark along
with, from Latin com- together (see <a href=/index.php?term=com-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>com-</a>) + notare to mark (see <a
href=/index.php?term=note&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>note</a>).  <br /><br />  A
word denotes its primary meaning, its barest adequate definition -- father denotes one that has
begotten. A word connotes the attributes commonly associated with it -- father connotes male sex,
prior existence, greater experience, affection, guidance.
