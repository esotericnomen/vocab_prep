mid-14c., ample, sizeable, from Old French substantiel (13c.) and directly from Latin substantialis
having substance or reality, material, in Late Latin pertaining to the substance or essence, from
substantia being, essence, material(see <a href=/index.php?term=substance&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>substance</a>). Meaning existing, having real existence is from late 14c.
Meaning involving an essential part or point is early 15c. Related: Substantially.
