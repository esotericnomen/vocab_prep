1540s, acceptable, agreeable, from Latin plausibilis deserving applause, acceptable, from plaus-,
past participle stem of plaudere to applaud (see <a
href=/index.php?term=plaudit&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>plaudit</a>). Meaning
having the appearance of truth is recorded from 1560s. Related: Plausibly.
