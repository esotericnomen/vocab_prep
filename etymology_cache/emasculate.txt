c.1600, from Latin emasculatus, past participle of emasculare castrate, from ex- out, away (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ex-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ex-</a>) + masculus male, manly
(see <a href=/index.php?term=masculine&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>masculine</a>).
Originally and usually in a figurative sense. Related: Emasculated; emasculating.
