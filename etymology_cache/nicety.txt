mid-14c., folly, stupidity, from Old French nicet&eacute; foolishness, childishness, simplicity,
from nice silly (see <a href=/index.php?term=nice&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>nice</a>). Underwent sense evolution parallel to nice, arriving at minute,
subtle point 1580s and exactitude in 1650s. Phrase to a nicety exactly is attested from 1795.
