1540s, from be- thoroughly + Middle English rate to scold (late 14c.), from Old French reter accuse,
blame, from Latin reputare (see <a href=/index.php?term=reputation&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>reputation</a>). Obsolete except in U.S. [OED 1st ed.], but it seems to have
revived in Britain 20c. Related: Berated; berating.
