1650s, trick, from French prestige (16c.) deceit, imposture, illusion (in Modern French, illusion,
magic, glamour), from Latin praestigium delusion, illusion (see <a
href=/index.php?term=prestigious&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>prestigious</a>).
Derogatory until 19c.; sense of dazzling influence first applied 1815, to Napoleon.
