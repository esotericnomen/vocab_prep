1590s, to sway unsteadily, from Latin vacillatus, past participle of vacillare sway to and fro;
hesitate (see <a href=/index.php?term=vacillation&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>vacillation</a>). Meaning to waver between two opinions or courses is recorded
from 1620s. Related: Vacillated; vacillates; vacillating.
