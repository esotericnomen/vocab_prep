1560s, disputation, from Late Latin litigationem (nominative litigatio), noun of action from past
participle stem of Latin litigare to dispute, quarrel, strive, from phrase litem agere, from litem
(nominative lis) lawsuit, dispute, quarrel, strife + agere to drive, conduct (see <a
href=/index.php?term=act&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>act</a>). Meaning act of
carrying on a lawsuit is from 1640s.
