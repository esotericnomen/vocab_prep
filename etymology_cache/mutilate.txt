1530s, of things; 1560s, of persons; from Latin mutilatus, past participle of mutilare to cut off,
lop off, cut short; maim, mutilate, from mutilus maimed (see <a
href=/index.php?term=mutilation&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>mutilation</a>).
Technically, to deprive of some principal part, especially by cutting off. Related: Mutilated;
mutilating.
