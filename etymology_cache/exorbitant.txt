mid-15c., a legal term, deviating from rule or principle, eccentric; from Latin exorbitantem
(nominative exorbitans), present participle of exorbitare deviate, go out of the track, from ex- out
of (see <a href=/index.php?term=ex-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ex-</a>) + orbita
wheel track (see <a href=/index.php?term=orb&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>orb</a>).
Sense of excessive, immoderate is from 1620s; of prices, rates, etc., from 1660s. Related:
Exorbitantly.
