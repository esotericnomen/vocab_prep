late 13c., from Old French diademe and directly from Latin diadema cloth band worn around the head
as a sign of royalty, from Greek diadema, from diadein to bind across, from dia- across (see <a
href=/index.php?term=dia-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>dia-</a>) + dein to bind,
related to desmos band, from PIE *de- to bind. Used of the headband worn by Persian kings and
adopted by Alexander the Great and his successors.
