1560s, an ellipse, from Latin ellipsis, from Greek elleipsis a falling short, defect, ellipse, from
elleipein to fall short, leave out, from en- in + leipein to leave (see <a
href=/index.php?term=relinquish&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>relinquish</a>).
Grammatical sense first recorded 1610s.
