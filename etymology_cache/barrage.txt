1859, action of barring; man-made barrier in a stream, from French barrer to stop, from barre bar,
from Old French barre (see <a href=/index.php?term=bar&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>bar</a> (n.1)). Artillery sense is 1916, from World War I French phrase tir de
barrage barrier fire intended to isolate the objective. As a verb by 1917. Related: Barraged;
barraging.
