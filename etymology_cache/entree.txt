1724, opening piece of an opera or ballet, from French entr&eacute;e, from Old French entree (see <a
href=/index.php?term=entry&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>entry</a>). Cookery sense is
from 1759; originally the dish which was introductory to the main course. The word had been borrowed
in Middle English as entre act of entering.
