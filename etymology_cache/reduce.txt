late 14c., bring back, from Old French reducer (14c.), from Latin reducere lead back, bring back,
figuratively restore, replace, from re- back (see <a href=/index.php?term=re-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>re-</a>) + ducere bring, lead (see <a
href=/index.php?term=duke&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>duke</a> (n.)). Meaning bring
to an inferior condition is 1570s; that of bring to a lower rank is 1640s (military reduce to ranks
is from 1802); that of subdue by force of arms is 1610s. Sense of to lower, diminish, lessen is from
1787. Related: Reduced; reducing.
