1570s, from Middle French convexe, from Latin convexus vaulted, arched, past participle of convehere
to bring together, from com- together, or thoroughly (see <a
href=/index.php?term=com-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>com-</a>) + vehere to bring
(see <a href=/index.php?term=vehicle&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>vehicle</a>).
Possibly from the idea of vaults carried together to meet at the point of a roof. Related:
Convexity. Convex lens is from 1822.
