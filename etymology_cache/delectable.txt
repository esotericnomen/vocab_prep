delectable (adj.)

late 14c., from Old French delectable, from Latin delectabilis delightful, from delectare (see
delight (n.)). Related: Delectably.


hautboy (n.)

oboe, double-reeded woodwind instrument, 1570s, from French hautbois high wood (15c.; see oboe,
which is the Italian phonetic spelling of the French word). The haut is used here in its secondary
sense of high-pitched. In early use frequently nativized as hoboy, hawboy, etc.This Pageaunt waz
clozd vp with a delectable harmony of Hautboiz, Shalmz, Coronets, and such oother looud muzik.
[Robert Laneham, 1575]
