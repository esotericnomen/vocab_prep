late 14c., a breaking into parts, from Old French resolution (14c.) or directly from Latin
resolutionem (nominative resolutio) process of reducing things into simpler forms, from past
participle stem of resolvere loosen (see <a href=/index.php?term=resolve&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>resolve</a>). Sense of a solving (as of mathematical problems) first recorded
1540s, as is that of power of holding firmly (compare <a
href=/index.php?term=resolute&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>resolute</a>). Sense of
decision or expression of a meeting is from c.1600. Meaning effect of an optical instrument is from
1860. New Year's resolutions in reference to a specific intention to better oneself is at least from
1780s, and through 19c. generally of a pious nature.
