c.1400, from Latin benedictionem (nominative benedictio), noun of action from bene dicere to speak
well of, bless, from bene well (see <a href=/index.php?term=bene-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>bene-</a>) + dicere to speak (see <a
href=/index.php?term=diction&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>diction</a>). The oldest
sense in English is of grace before meat. The older French form, benei&ccedil;on passed into Middle
English as <a href=/index.php?term=benison&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>benison</a>.
