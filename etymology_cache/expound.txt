c.1300, from Old French espondre expound (on), set forth, explain, from Latin exponere put forth,
explain, expose, exhibit, from ex- forth (see <a href=/index.php?term=ex-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>ex-</a>) + ponere to put, place (see <a
href=/index.php?term=position&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>position</a>); with
intrusive -d (compare <a href=/index.php?term=sound&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>sound</a> (n.1)). The usual Middle English form was expoune. Related:
Expounded; expounding.
