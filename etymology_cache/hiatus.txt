1560s, break or opening in a material object, from Latin hiatus opening, aperture, rupture, gap,
from past participle stem of hiare to gape, stand open (see <a
href=/index.php?term=yawn&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>yawn</a> (v.)). Sense of gap
or interruption in events, etc. is first recorded 1610s.
