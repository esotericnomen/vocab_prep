1610s, deductive reasoning, from Latin synthesis collection, set, suit of clothes, composition (of a
medication), from Greek synthesis composition, a putting together, from syntithenai put together,
combine, from syn- together (see <a href=/index.php?term=syn-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>syn-</a>) + tithenai put, place (see <a
href=/index.php?term=theme&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>theme</a>). From 1733 as a
combination of parts into a whole. Earlier borrowed in Middle English as sintecis (mid-15c.). Plural
syntheses.
