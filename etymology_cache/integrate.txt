1630s, to render (something) whole, from Latin integratus, past participle of integrare make whole,
from integer whole (see <a href=/index.php?term=integer&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>integer</a>). Meaning to put together parts or elements and combine them into a
whole is from 1802. Integrate in the racially desegregate sense is a back-formation from
integration, dating to the 1948 U.S. presidential contest. Related: <a
href=/index.php?term=Integrated&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>Integrated</a>;
integrating.
