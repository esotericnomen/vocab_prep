1540s, from Latin dubiosus doubtful, from dubium doubt, neuter of dubius vacillating, moving two
ways, fluctuating; figuratively wavering in opinion, doubting, doubtful, from duo two (see <a
href=/index.php?term=two&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>two</a>), with a sense of of
two minds, undecided between two things. Old English also used tweo two to mean doubt. Compare <a
href=/index.php?term=doubt&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>doubt</a> (v.). Related:
Dubiously; dubiousness.
