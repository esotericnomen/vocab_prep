1650s, from Greek polyglottos speaking many languages, literally many-tongued, from polys many (see
<a href=/index.php?term=poly-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>poly-</a>) + glotta, Attic
variant of glossa language, literally tongue (see <a
href=/index.php?term=gloss&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>gloss</a> (n.2)). As a noun
from 1640s.
