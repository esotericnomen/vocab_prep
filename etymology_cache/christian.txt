16c., forms replacing earlier Christen, from Old English cristen (noun and adjective), from a West
Germanic borrowing of Church Latin christianus, from Ecclesiastical Greek christianos, from Christos
(see <a href=/index.php?term=Christ&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>Christ</a>). First
used in Antioch, according to Acts xi:25-26. Christian Science as the name of a religious sect is
from 1863.
