anticlimax (n.)

the addition of a particular which suddenly lowers the effect, 1701, from anti- + climax (n.).


bathos (n.)

anticlimax, a descent from the sublime to the ridiculous, 1727, from Greek bathos depth, related to
bathys deep (see benthos). Introduced by Pope.


anticlimactic (adj.)

also anti-climactic, 1831; see anticlimax + -ic.
