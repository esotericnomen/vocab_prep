late 15c., from Old French succomber succumb, die, lose one's (legal) case, and directly from Latin
succumbere submit, surrender, yield, be overcome; sink down; lie under; cohabit with, from sub down
(see <a href=/index.php?term=sub-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>sub-</a>) + -cumbere
take a reclining position, related to cubare lie down (see <a
href=/index.php?term=cubicle&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>cubicle</a>). Originally
transitive; sense of sink under pressure is first recorded c.1600. As a euphemism for to die, from
1849. Related: Succumbed; succumbing.
