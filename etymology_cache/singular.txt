mid-14c., alone, apart; being a unit; special, unsurpassed, from Old French singuler personal
particular; distinctive; singular in number (12c., Modern French singulier) or directly from Latin
singularis single, solitary, one by one, one at a time; peculiar, remarkable, from singulus (see <a
href=/index.php?term=single&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>single</a> (adj.)). Meaning
remarkably good, unusual, rare, separated from others (by excellence), uncommon is from c.1400 in
English; this also was a common meaning of Latin singularis.
