circuitous (adj.)

1660s, from Medieval Latin circuitous full of roundabout ways, from Latin circuitus a going round
(see circuit (n.)). Related: Circuitously; circuitousness.


roundabout (adv.)

by a circuitous route, mid-14c., from round (adv.) + about. As an adjective from c.1600. Noun sense
of traffic circle is attested from 1927.
