class=crossreference>swipe</a> (n.). The slang sense of steal, pilfer appeared 1885, American
English; earliest use in prison jargon:  <blockquote>  The blokes in the next cell, little Charley
Ames and the Sheeney Kid, they was hot to try it, and swiped enough shoe-lining out of shop No. 5,
where they worked, to make us all breeches to the stripes. [Lippincott's Magazine, vol. 35, June
1885]  </blockquote>  Meaning run a credit card is 1990s. Related: Swiped; swiper; swiping.
