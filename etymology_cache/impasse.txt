1851, blind alley, from French impasse impassable road, blind alley, impasse, from assimilated form
of in- not, opposite of (see <a href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>in-</a> (1)) + Middle French passe a passing, from passer to pass (see <a
href=/index.php?term=pass&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>pass</a> (v.)). Supposedly
coined by Voltaire as a euphemism for cul de sac. Figurative use also from 1851.
