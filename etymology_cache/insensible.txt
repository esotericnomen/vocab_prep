c.1400, lacking the power to feel with the senses, from Latin insensibilis that cannot be felt, from
in- not (see <a href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (1)) +
sensibilis (see <a href=/index.php?term=sensible&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>sensible</a>). Also sometimes in Middle English incapable of being felt or
perceived by the senses (early 15c.). Meaning unconscious is attested from early 15c. See <a
href=/index.php?term=insensate&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>insensate</a>.
