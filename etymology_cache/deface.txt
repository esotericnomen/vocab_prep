defacement (n.)

1560s, from deface + -ment.


deface (v.)

mid-14c., to obliterate, from Old French desfacier mutilate, destroy, disfigure, from des- away from
(see dis-) + Vulgar Latin *facia (see face (n.)). Weaker sense of to mar, make ugly is late 14c. in
English. Related: Defaced; defacing.


efface (v.)

late 15c., from Middle French effacer, from Old French esfacier (12c.) to wipe out, destroy,
literally to remove the face, from es- out (see ex-) + face appearance, from Latin facies face (see
face (n.)). Related: Effaced; effacing. Compare deface.
