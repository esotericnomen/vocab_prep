late 15c., deception, false statement, from Latin fallacia deception, noun of quality from fallax
(genitive fallacis) deceptive, from fallere deceive (see <a
href=/index.php?term=fail&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>fail</a> (v.)). Specific sense
in logic dates from 1550s. An earlier form was fallace (c.1300), from Old French fallace.
