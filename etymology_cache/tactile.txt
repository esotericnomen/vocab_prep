1610s, perceptible to touch, from French tactile (16c.) and directly from Latin tactilis tangible,
that may be touched, from tactus, past participle of tangere to touch (see <a
href=/index.php?term=tangent&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>tangent</a> (adj.)).
Meaning of or pertaining to the sense of touch is attested from 1650s. Related: Tactility.
