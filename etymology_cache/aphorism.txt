1520s (especially in reference to the Aphorisms of Hippocrates), from Middle French aphorisme (14c.,
aufforisme), from Late Latin aphorismus, from Greek aphorismos definition, pithy sentence, from
aphorizein to mark off, divide, from apo- from (see <a
href=/index.php?term=apo-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>apo-</a>) + horizein to bound
(see <a href=/index.php?term=horizon&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>horizon</a>).  <br
/><br />  An aphorism is a short, pithy statement containing a truth of general import; an <a
href=/index.php?term=axiom&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>axiom</a> is a statement of
self-evident truth; a <a href=/index.php?term=theorem&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>theorem</a> is a demonstrable proposition in science or mathematics; an <a
href=/index.php?term=epigram&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>epigram</a> is like an
aphorism, but lacking in general import. <a href=/index.php?term=Maxim&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>Maxim</a> and <a href=/index.php?term=saying&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>saying</a> can be used as synonyms for aphorism.
