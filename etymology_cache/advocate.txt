mid-14c., one whose profession is to plead cases in a court of justice, a technical term from Roman
law, from Old French avocat barrister, advocate, spokesman, from Latin advocatus one called to aid;
a pleader, advocate, noun use of past participle of advocare to call (as witness or advisor) from
ad- to (see <a href=/index.php?term=ad-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ad-</a>) +
vocare to call, related to vocem (see <a href=/index.php?term=voice&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>voice</a> (n.)). Also in Middle English as one who intercedes for another, and
protector, champion, patron. Feminine forms advocatess, advocatrice were in use in 15c.
