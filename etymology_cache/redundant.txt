1590s, from Latin redundantem (nominative redundans), present participle of redundare, literally
overflow, pour over; be over-full; figuratively be in excess, from re- again (see <a
href=/index.php?term=re-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>re-</a>) + undare rise in
waves, from unda a wave (see <a href=/index.php?term=water&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>water</a> (n.1)). Of persons, in employment situations, from 1928, chiefly
British. Related: Redundantly.
