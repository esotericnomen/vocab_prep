early 15c., repeat, from Latin replicatus, past participle of replicare (see <a
href=/index.php?term=reply&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>reply</a>). Meaning to copy,
reproduce, make a replica of is from 1882, a back-formation from <a
href=/index.php?term=replication&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>replication</a>.
Genetic sense is first recorded 1957. Related: Replicated; replicating.
