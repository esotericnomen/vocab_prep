1590s, from Late Latin entitatem (nominative entitas), from ens (genitive entis) a thing, proposed
by Caesar as present participle of esse be (see <a href=/index.php?term=is&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>is</a>), to render Greek philosophical term to on that which is (from neuter of
present participle of einai to be; see <a href=/index.php?term=essence&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>essence</a>). Originally abstract; concrete sense in English is from 1620s.
