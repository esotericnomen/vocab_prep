1510s, from Late Latin insensatus irrational, foolish, from Latin in- not (see <a
href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (1)) + sensatus gifted
with sense (see <a href=/index.php?term=sensate&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>sensate</a>). Insensate means not capable of feeling sensation, often
inanimate; <a href=/index.php?term=insensible&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>insensible</a> means lacking the power to feel with the senses, hence, often,
unconscious; <a href=/index.php?term=insensitive&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>insensitive</a> means having little or no reaction to what is perceived by
one's senses, often tactless.
