mid-14c., everyday, daily, from Old French cotidian (Modern French quotidien), from Latin
quotidianus daily, from Latin quotus how many? which in order or number? (see <a
href=/index.php?term=quote&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>quote</a> (v.)) + dies day
(see <a href=/index.php?term=diurnal&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>diurnal</a>).
Meaning ordinary, commonplace, trivial is from mid-15c.
