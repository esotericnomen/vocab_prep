early 15c., inscisif, slashing, cutting with a sharp edge, from Middle French incisif and directly
from Medieval Latin incisivus, from Latin incis-, past participle stem of incidere (see <a
href=/index.php?term=incision&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>incision</a>). Originally
literal; figurative sense of mentally acute first recorded 1850 as a borrowing from French. Related:
Incisively; incisiveness.
