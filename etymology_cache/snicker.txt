snicker (n.)

a smothered laugh, 1835, from snicker (v.).


snicker (v.)

1690s, possibly of imitative origin, similar to Dutch snikken to gasp, sob. Related: Snickered;
snickering.


snigger (v.)

1706, variant form of snicker (v.). Related: Sniggered; sniggering. As a noun from 1823.


whicker (v.)

1650s, snigger, imitative (compare snicker). As imitative of a sound made by a horse, from 1753. As
the sound of something beating the air, from 1920. Related: Whickered; whickering.
