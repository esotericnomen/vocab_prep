mid-14c., colrik, bilious of temperament or complexion, from Old French colerique, from Late Latin
cholericus, from Greek kholerikos (see <a href=/index.php?term=choler&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>choler</a>). Meaning easily angered, hot-tempered is from 1580s (from the
supposed effect of excess choler); that of pertaining to cholera is from 1834.
