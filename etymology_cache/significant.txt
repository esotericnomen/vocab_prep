1570s, having a meaning, from Latin significantem (nominative significans, present participle of
significare make known, indicate (see <a href=/index.php?term=signify&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>signify</a>). Earlier in the same sense was significative (c.1400). Often
having a special or secret meaning, hence important (1761). Related: Significantly. Significant
figure is from 1680s. Significant other (n.) attested by 1961, in psychology, the most influential
other person in the patient's world.
