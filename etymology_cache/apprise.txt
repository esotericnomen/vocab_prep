apprise (v.)

to notify, 1690s, from French appris, past participle of apprendre to inform, teach, literally to
lay hold of (in the mind), another metaphoric meaning of Latin apprehendere (see apprehend).
Related: Apprised; apprising.


notify (v.)

late 14c., from Old French notefiier make known, inform, apprise (13c.), from Latin notificare to
make known, notify, from Latin notus known (see notice (n.)) + root of facere make, do (see
factitious). Related: Notified; notifying.
