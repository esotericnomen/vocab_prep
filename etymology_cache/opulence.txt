opulence (n.)

c.1510, from Middle French opulence (16c.), from Latin opulentia, from opulentus wealthy,
dissimilated from *op-en-ent-, related to ops wealth, power, resources, opus work, labor, exertion,
from PIE root *op- (1) to work, produce in abundance (see opus).


opulent (adj.)

c.1600, from Middle French opulent, from Latin opulentem (nominative opulens) wealthy, rich, from
opulentus (see opulence).


riches (n.)

valued possessions, money, property, c.1200, modified from richesse (12c.), a singular form
misunderstood as a plural, from Old French richesse, richece wealth, opulence, splendor,
magnificence, from riche (see rich (adj.)). The Old French suffix -esse is from Latin -itia, added
to adjectives to form nouns of quality (compare duress, largesse).
