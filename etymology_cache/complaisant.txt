1640s, from French complaisant (16c.), in Middle French, pleasing, present participle of complaire
acquiesce to please, from Latin complacere be very pleasing (see <a
href=/index.php?term=complacent&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>complacent</a>, with
which it overlapped till mid-19c.). Possibly influenced in French by Old French plaire gratify.
