1640s, evergreen, formed in English from Latin perennis lasting through the year (or years), from
per- through (see <a href=/index.php?term=per&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>per</a>) +
annus year (see <a href=/index.php?term=annual&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>annual</a>). Botanical sense of Remaining alive through a number of years is
attested from 1670s; figurative meaning of enduring, permanent is from 1750. Related: Perennially.
For vowel change, see <a href=/index.php?term=biennial&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>biennial</a>. The noun meaning a perennial plant is from 1763.
