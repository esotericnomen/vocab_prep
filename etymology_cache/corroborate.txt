1530s, to give (legal) confirmation to, from Latin corroboratus, past participle of corroborare to
strengthen, invigorate, from com- together or thoroughly (see <a
href=/index.php?term=com-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>com-</a>) + roborare to make
strong, from  robur, robus strength, (see <a href=/index.php?term=robust&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>robust</a>).  <br /><br />  Meaning to strengthen by evidence, to confirm is
from 1706. Sometimes in early use the word also has its literal Latin sense, especially of
medicines. Related: Corroborated; corroborating; corroborative.
