1650s, having to do with prose, from Middle French prosaique and directly from Medieval Latin
prosaicus in prose (16c.), from Latin prosa prose (see <a
href=/index.php?term=prose&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>prose</a>). Meaning having
the character of prose (in contrast to the feeling of poetry) is by 1746; extended sense of ordinary
is by 1813, both from French.
