late 14c., from Latin pigmentum coloring matter, pigment, paint, figuratively prnament, from stem of
pingere to color, paint (see <a href=/index.php?term=paint&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>paint</a> (v.)). Variants of this word could have been known in Old English
(compare 12c. pyhmentum). As a verb from 1900. Related: Pigmented; pigmenting.
