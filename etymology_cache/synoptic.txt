1763, pertaining to synopsis, from Modern Latin synopticus, from Late Latin synopsis (see <a
href=/index.php?term=synopsis&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>synopsis</a>). Greek
synoptikos meant taking a general or comprehensive view, and the sense affording a general view in
English emerged by mid-19c. Specifically of the first three Gospels from 1841, on notion of giving
an account of events from the same point of view. Related Synoptical (1660s).
