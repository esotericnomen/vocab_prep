mid-14c., unconquerable, from Latin insuperabilis that cannot be passed over, unconquerable, from
in- not, opposite of (see <a href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>in-</a> (1)) + superabilis that may be overcome, from superare to overcome,
from superus one that is above, from super over (see <a
href=/index.php?term=super-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>super-</a>). Figurative use
from 1650s. Related: Insuperably.
