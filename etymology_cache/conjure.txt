late 13c., command on oath, from Old French conjurer invoke, conjure (12c.), from Latin coniurare to
swear together; conspire, from com- together (see <a
href=/index.php?term=com-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>com-</a>) + iurare to swear
(see <a href=/index.php?term=jury&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>jury</a> (n.)).
Magical sense is c.1300, for constraining by spell a demon to do one's bidding. Related: Conjured;
conjuring. Phrase conjure up cause to appear in the mind (as if by magic) attested from 1580s.
