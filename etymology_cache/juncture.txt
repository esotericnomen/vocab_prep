late 14c., place where two things are joined, from Latin iunctura a joining, uniting, a joint, from
iunctus, past participle of iungere to join (see <a
href=/index.php?term=jugular&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>jugular</a>). Sense of
point in time first recorded 1650s, probably from astrology.
