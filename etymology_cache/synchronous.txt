1660s, existing or happening at the same time, from Late Latin synchronus simultaneous, from Greek
synkhronos happening at the same time, from syn- together (see <a
href=/index.php?term=syn-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>syn-</a>) + khronos time (see
<a href=/index.php?term=chrono-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>chrono-</a>). Meaning
recurring at the same successive instants of time is attested from 1670s. Related: Synchronously.
