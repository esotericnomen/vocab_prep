1660s, elegant, stylish, from French gentil nice, pleasing, in Old French noble (see <a
href=/index.php?term=gentle&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>gentle</a>). Form reflects
attempt to render the French pronunciation of gentil. Meaning easy and sprightly in manner first
attested 1670s. Related: Jauntily; jauntiness.
