private, early 13c., from Old French priv&eacute; friendly, intimate; a private place, from Latin
privatus private, personal (see <a href=/index.php?term=private&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>private</a> (adj.)). Meaning participating in (a secret) (usually with to) is
attested from late 14c. Related: Privily. Privy Council is from c.1300 in a general sense;
specifically of the British government, first attested late 14c., as consaile priue. Privy member
organ of sex is from late 13c.
