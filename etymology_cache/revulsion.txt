1540s, as a medical term, from Middle French revulsion (16c.) or directly from Latin revulsionem
(nominative revulsio) a tearing off, act of pulling away, noun of action from past participle stem
of revellere to pull away, from re- away (see <a href=/index.php?term=re-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>re-</a>) + vellere to tear, pull, from PIE *wel-no-, suffixed form of root
*wel- (4) to tear, pull (see <a href=/index.php?term=svelte&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>svelte</a>). The meaning sudden reaction of disgust is first attested 1816.
