late 14c., one who has held an office or position before the present holder, from Old French
predecesseor forebear and directly from Late Latin praedecessorem (nominative praedecessor), from
Latin prae before (see <a href=/index.php?term=pre-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>pre-</a>) + decessor retiring official, from decess-, past participle stem of
decedere go away, also die (see <a href=/index.php?term=decease&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>decease</a> (n.)). Meaning ancestor, forefather is recorded from c.1400.
