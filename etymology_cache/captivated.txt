1520s, to enthrall with charm, from Late Latin captivatus, past participle of captivare to take,
capture, from captivus (see <a href=/index.php?term=captive&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>captive</a>). Literal sense (1550s) is rare or obsolete in English, which uses
<a href=/index.php?term=capture&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>capture</a> (q.v.).
Latin captare to take, hold also had a transferred sense of to entice, entrap, allure. Related:
Captivated; captivating; captivatingly.
