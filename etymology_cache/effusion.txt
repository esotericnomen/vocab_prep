c.1400, a pouring out, from Middle French effusion (14c.) and directly from Latin effusionem
(nominative effusio) a pouring forth, noun of action from past participle stem of effundere pour
forth, spread abroad, from ex- out (see <a href=/index.php?term=ex-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>ex-</a>) + fundere pour (see <a
href=/index.php?term=found&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>found</a> (v.2)).
Figuratively, of speech, emotion, etc., from 1650s.
