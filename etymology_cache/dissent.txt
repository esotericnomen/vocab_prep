dissent (v.)

early 15c., from Latin dissentire differ in sentiments, disagree, be at odds, contradict, quarrel,
from dis- differently (see dis-) + sentire to feel, think (see sense (n.)). Related: Dissented;
dissenting. The noun is 1580s, from the verb.  <blockquote>Has there ever been a society which has
died of dissent? Several have died of conformity in our lifetime. [Jacob Bronowski Science and Human
Values, 1956]</blockquote>
