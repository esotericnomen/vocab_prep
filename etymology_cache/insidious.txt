1540s, from Middle French insidieux (15c.) or directly from Latin insidiosus deceitful, cunning,
artful, from insidiae (plural) plot, snare, ambush, from insidere sit on, occupy, from in- in (see
<a href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (2)) + sedere to sit
(see <a href=/index.php?term=sedentary&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>sedentary</a>).
Related: Insidiously; insidiousness.
