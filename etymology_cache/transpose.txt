late 14c., from Old French transposer transfer, remove; present, render symbolically (14c.), from
Latin transponere (past participle transpositus) to place over, set over, from trans- over (see <a
href=/index.php?term=trans-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>trans-</a>) + ponere to put,
place (see <a href=/index.php?term=position&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>position</a>
(n.)). Form altered in French on model of poser to put, place. Sense of put music in a different key
is from c.1600. Related: Transposed; transposing.
