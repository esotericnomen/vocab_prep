late 13c., dwelling place of a hermit, from Old French hermitage, from Latin heremite (see <a
href=/index.php?term=hermit&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>hermit</a>). Earlier in the
same sense was hermitorie (c.1200), from Medieval Latin hermitorium. Transferred sense of solitary
or secluded dwelling place is from 1640s.
