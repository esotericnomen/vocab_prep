1690s, sign language, from Greek semeion a sign, mark, token, from sema (compare <a
href=/index.php?term=semiotic&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>semiotic</a>) + <a
href=/index.php?term=-ology&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>-ology</a>. As branch of
medical science concerned with symptoms, 1839; as logical theory of signs from 1923. Related:
Semiological.
