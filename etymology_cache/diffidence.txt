c.1400, from Latin diffidentia mistrust, distrust, want of confidence, from diffidere to mistrust,
lack confidence, from dis- away (see <a href=/index.php?term=dis-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>dis-</a>) + fidere to trust (see <a
href=/index.php?term=faith&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>faith</a>). Modern sense is
of distrusting oneself (1650s). The original sense was the opposite of confidence.
