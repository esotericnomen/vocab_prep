mid-15c., from <a href=/index.php?term=wind&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>wind</a>
(n.1) + <a href=/index.php?term=fall&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>fall</a> (n.1).
Originally literal, in reference to wood or fruit blown down by the wind, and thus free to all.
Figurative sense of unexpected acquisition is recorded from 1540s.
