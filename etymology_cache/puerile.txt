1660s, youthful, boyish, a back-formation from puerility, or else from French pu&#233;ril (15c.),
from Latin puerilis boyish; childish, from puer boy, child (see <a
href=/index.php?term=puerility&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>puerility</a>).
Disparaging sense, juvenile, immature, is from 1680s.
