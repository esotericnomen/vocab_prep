1520s, from Latin insinuatus, past participle of insinuare to throw in, push in, make a way; creep
in, intrude, bring in by windings and curvings, wind one's way into, from in- in (see <a
href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (2)) + sinuare to wind,
bend, curve, from sinus a curve, winding (see <a href=/index.php?term=sinus&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>sinus</a>). Sense of to introduce tortuously or indirectly is from 1640s.
Related: Insinuated; insinuating; insinuatingly.
