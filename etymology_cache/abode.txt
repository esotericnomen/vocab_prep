mid-13c., action of waiting, verbal noun identical with Old English abad, past participle of abiden
to abide (see <a href=/index.php?term=abide&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>abide</a>),
used as a verbal noun. The present-to-preterite vowel change is consistent with an Old English class
I strong verb (ride/rode, etc.). Meaning habitual residence is first attested 1570s.
