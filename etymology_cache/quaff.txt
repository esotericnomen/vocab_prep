quaff (v.)

1510s (implied in quaffer), perhaps imitative, or perhaps from Low German quassen to overindulge (in
food and drink), with -ss- misread as -ff-. Related: Quaffed; quaffing. The noun is attested by
1570s, from the verb.


carouse (v.)

1550s, from Middle French carousser drink, quaff, swill, from German gar aus quite out, from gar
austrinken; trink garaus to drink up entirely. Frequently also as an adverb in early English usage
(to drink carouse).
