late 15c., austerity or strictness of life, from Middle French severite, from Latin severitas
seriousness, strictness, sternness, from severus stern, strict, serious, of uncertain origin.
Possibly from PIE root *segh- to have, hold (see <a
href=/index.php?term=scheme&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>scheme</a> (n.)), or
possibly from *se vero without kindness, from se without (see <a
href=/index.php?term=secret&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>secret</a>) + *vero
kindness, neuter ablative of verus true (see <a href=/index.php?term=very&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>very</a>). Meaning strictness in dealing with others is recorded from 1520s.
