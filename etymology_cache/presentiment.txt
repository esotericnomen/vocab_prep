1714, from obsolete French presentiment (Modern French Related: pressentiment), from Middle French
pressentir to have foreboding, from Latin praesentire to sense beforehand, from prae before (see <a
href=/index.php?term=pre-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>pre-</a>) + sentire perceive,
feel (see <a href=/index.php?term=sense&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>sense</a> (n.)).
