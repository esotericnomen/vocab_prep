early 15c., from Late Latin inanimatus lifeless, from in- not (see <a
href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (1)) + animatus (see <a
href=/index.php?term=animation&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>animation</a>). The same
word in 17c. also was a verb meaning to infuse with life, from the other in- (see <a
href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (2)).
