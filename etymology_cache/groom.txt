1809, from <a href=/index.php?term=groom&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>groom</a> (n.1)
in its secondary sense of male servant who attends to horses. Transferred sense of to tidy (oneself)
up is from 1843; figurative sense of to prepare a candidate is from 1887, originally in U.S.
politics. Related: Groomed; grooming.
