early 14c., from Old French omnipotent almighty, all-powerful (11c.) or directly from Latin
omnipotentem (nominative omnipotens) all-powerful, almighty, from omnis all (see <a
href=/index.php?term=omni&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>omni</a>-) + potens (genitive
potentis) powerful (see <a href=/index.php?term=potent&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>potent</a>). Strictly only of God or a deity; general sense of having absolute
power or authority is attested from 1590s.
