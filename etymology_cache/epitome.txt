1520s, an abstract; brief statement of the chief points of some writing, from Middle French
&eacute;pitom&eacute; (16c.), from Latin epitome abridgment, from Greek epitome abridgment, from
epitemnein cut short, abridge, from epi into (see <a
href=/index.php?term=epi-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>epi-</a>) + temnein to cut
(see <a href=/index.php?term=tome&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>tome</a>). Sense of
person or thing that typifies something is first recorded c.1600.
