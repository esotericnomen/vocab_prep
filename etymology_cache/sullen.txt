1570s, alteration of Middle English soleyn unique, singular, from Anglo-French *solein, formed on
the pattern of Old French solain lonely, from soul single, from Latin solus by oneself, alone (see
<a href=/index.php?term=sole&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>sole</a> (adj.)). The sense
shift in Middle English from solitary to morose (i.e. remaining alone through ill-humor) occurred
late 14c. Related: Sullenly; sullenness.
