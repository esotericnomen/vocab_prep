1650s, pale, from Latin luridus pale yellow, ghastly, of uncertain origin, perhaps cognate with
Greek khloros (see <a href=/index.php?term=Chloe&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>Chloe</a>). Meaning glowing in the darkness is from 1727. The figurative sense
of sensational is first attested 1850. Related: Luridly.
