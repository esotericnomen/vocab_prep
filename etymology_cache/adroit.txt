1650s, dexterous, originally rightly, from French adroit, from phrase &agrave; droit according to
right, from Old French &agrave; to (see <a href=/index.php?term=ad-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>ad-</a>) + droit right, from Late Latin directum right, justice, accusative of
Latin directus straight (see <a href=/index.php?term=direct&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>direct</a> (v.)). Related: Adroitly; adroitness.
