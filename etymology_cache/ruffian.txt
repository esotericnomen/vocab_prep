1530s, a boisterous, brutal fellow, one ready to commit any crime, from Middle French rufian a pimp
(15c.), from Italian ruffiano a pander, pimp, of uncertain origin, perhaps from a Germanic source
related to <a href=/index.php?term=rough&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>rough</a>
(adj.), but Dutch roffiaan, German Ruffian are said to be from French. English meaning might have
been influenced by similarity of sound to rough. Related: Ruffianly.  <br /><br />  The Romanic
words (such as Medieval Latin ruffianus, Proven&ccedil;al rufian, Catalan rufia, Spanish rufian)
preserve the sense of protector or owner of whores. For sense evolution in English, compare <a
href=/index.php?term=bully&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>bully</a> (n.).
