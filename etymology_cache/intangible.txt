1630s, incapable of being touched, from French intangible (c.1500) or directly from Medieval Latin
intangibilis, from in- not (see <a href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>in-</a> (1)) + Late Latin tangibilis that may be touched (see <a
href=/index.php?term=tangible&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>tangible</a>). Figurative
sense of that cannot be grasped by the mind is from 1880. Noun meaning anything intangible is from
1914. Related: Intangibly.
