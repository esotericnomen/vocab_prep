late 14c., to enfold, enwrap, entangle (the classical Latin sense), from Old French emplier, from
Latin implicare involve (see <a href=/index.php?term=implication&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>implication</a>). Meaning to involve something unstated as a logical
consequence first recorded c.1400; that of to hint at from 1580s. Related: Implied; implying. The
distinction between imply and <a href=/index.php?term=infer&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>infer</a> is in What do you imply by that remark? But, What am I to infer from
that remark?
