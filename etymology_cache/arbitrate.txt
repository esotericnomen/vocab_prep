1580s (arbitrable is recorded from 1530s), to give an authoritative decision, from Latin arbitratus,
past participle of arbitrari be of an opinion, give a decision, from arbiter (see <a
href=/index.php?term=arbiter&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>arbiter</a>). Meaning to
act as an arbitrator is from 1610s. Related: Arbitrated; arbitrating. The earlier verb form was
arbitren (early 15c.).
