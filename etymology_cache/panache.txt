1550s, a tuft or plume of feathers, from Middle French pennache tuft of feathers, from Italian
pennaccio, from Late Latin pinnaculum small wing, gable, peak (see <a
href=/index.php?term=pinnacle&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>pinnacle</a>). Figurative
sense of display, swagger first recorded 1898 (in translation of Cyrano de Bergerac), from French.
