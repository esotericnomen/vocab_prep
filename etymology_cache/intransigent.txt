1881, from French intransigeant, from Spanish los intransigentes, literally those not coming to
agreement, name for extreme republican party in the Spanish Cortes 1873-4, from in- not (see <a
href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (1)) + transigente
compromising, from Latin transigentem (nominative transigens), present participle of transigere come
to an agreement, accomplish, to carry through (see <a
href=/index.php?term=transaction&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>transaction</a>).
Acquired its generalized sense in French.
