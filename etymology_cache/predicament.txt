early 15c., category, class; one of Aristotle's 10 categories, from Medieval Latin predicamentum,
from Late Latin praedicamentum quality, category, something predicted, that which is asserted, from
Latin praedicatus, past participle of praedicare (see <a
href=/index.php?term=predicate&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>predicate</a>).
Praedicamentum is a loan-translation of Greek kategoria, Aristotle's word. The meaning unpleasant
situation is first recorded 1580s.
