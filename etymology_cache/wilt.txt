1690s, to fade, droop, wither, probably an alteration of welk to wilt, probably from Middle Dutch or
Middle Low German welken to wither, cognate with Old High German irwelhen become soft, from Proto-
Germanic *welk-, from PIE root *welg- wet (see <a href=/index.php?term=welkin&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>welkin</a>). Transitive sense of cause to fade or droop is from 1809. Related:
Wilted; wilting.
