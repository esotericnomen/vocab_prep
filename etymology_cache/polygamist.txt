1590s, from Late Latin polygamia, from Late Greek polygamia polygamy, from polygamos often married,
from polys many + gamos marriage (see <a href=/index.php?term=gamete&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>gamete</a>). Not etymologically restricted to marriage of one man and multiple
women (technically <a href=/index.php?term=polygyny&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>polygyny</a>), but often used as if it were. Related: Polygamist.
