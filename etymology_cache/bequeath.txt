Old English becwe&eth;an to say, speak to, exhort, blame, also leave by will; from <a
href=/index.php?term=be-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>be-</a> + cwe&eth;an to say,
from Proto-Germanic *kwithan, from PIE *gwet- to say, speak.  <br /><br />  Original sense of say,
utter died out 13c., leaving legal sense of transfer by will. Closely related to <a
href=/index.php?term=bequest&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>bequest</a>. An old word
kept alive in wills [OED 1st ed.]. Old English beque&eth;ere meant interpreter, translator. Related:
Bequeathed; bequeathing.
