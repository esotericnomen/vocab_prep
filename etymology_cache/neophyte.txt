new convert, 1550s, from Ecclesiastical Latin neophytus, from Greek neophytos a new convert, noun
use of adjective meaning newly initiated, newly converted, literally newly planted, from neos new
(see <a href=/index.php?term=new&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>new</a>) + phytos
grown; planted, verbal adjective of phyein cause to grow, beget, plant (see <a
href=/index.php?term=physic&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>physic</a>). Church sense is
from I Tim. iii:6. Rare before 19c. General sense of one who is new to any subject is first recorded
1590s.
