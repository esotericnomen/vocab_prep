1667, Pand&aelig;monium, in Paradise Lost the name of the palace built in the middle of Hell, the
high capital of Satan and all his peers, coined by John Milton (1608-1674) from Greek pan- all (see
<a href=/index.php?term=pan-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>pan-</a>) + Late Latin
daemonium evil spirit, from Greek daimonion inferior divine power, from daimon lesser god (see <a
href=/index.php?term=demon&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>demon</a>).  <br /><br />
Transferred sense place of uproar is from 1779; that of wild, lawless confusion is from 1865.
Related: Pandemoniac; pandemoniacal; pandemonian; pandemonic.
