mid-15c., from Medieval Latin incompatibilis, from in- not, opposite of (see <a
href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (1)) + compatibilis
(see <a href=/index.php?term=compatible&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>compatible</a>).
Originally of benefices, incapable of being held together; sense of mutually intolerant is from
1590s. Related: Incompatibly.
