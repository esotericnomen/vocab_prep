mid-14c., having the same parents, derived from <a
href=/index.php?term=german&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>german</a> (adj.); compare
human/humane, urban/urbane. Main modern sense of closely connected, relevant (c.1600) derives from
use in Hamlet Act V, Scene ii: The phrase would bee more Germaine to the matter: If we could carry
Cannon by our sides, which is a figurative use of the word in the now-obsolete sense of closely
related, akin (late 15c.) in reference to things, not persons.
