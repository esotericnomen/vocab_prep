1789, from Latin avunculus maternal uncle, diminutive of avus (see <a
href=/index.php?term=uncle&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>uncle</a>) + <a
href=/index.php?term=-ar&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>-ar</a>. Used humorously for of
a pawnbroker (uncle was slang for pawnbroker from c.1600 through 19c.).<blockquote>My only good suit
is at present under the avuncular protection. [Fraser's Magazine, 1832]</blockquote>
