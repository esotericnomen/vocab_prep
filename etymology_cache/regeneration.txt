mid-14c., from Late Latin regenerationem (nominative regeneratio) a being born again, noun of action
from past participle stem of Latin regenerare make over, generate again, from re- again (see <a
href=/index.php?term=re-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>re-</a>) + generare to produce
(see <a href=/index.php?term=generation&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>generation</a>).
Originally spiritual; of animal tissue, early 15c.; of forests, 1888.
