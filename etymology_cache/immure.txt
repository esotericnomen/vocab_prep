1580s, from Middle French emmurer and directly from Medieval Latin immurare, literally to shut up
within walls, from assimilated form of in- into, in (see <a
href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (2)) + Latin murus wall
(see <a href=/index.php?term=mural&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>mural</a>). Related:
Immured; immuring.
