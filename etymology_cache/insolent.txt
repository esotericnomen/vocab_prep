late 14c., contemptuous, arrogant, haughty, from Latin insolentem (nominative insolens) arrogant,
immoderate, literally unusual, from in- not, opposite of (see <a
href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (1)) + solentem,
present participle of solere be accustomed, which possibly is related to sodalis close companion,
and to suescere become used to. Meaning contemptuous of rightful authority is from 1670s. Related:
Insolently.
