1590s, boiling, from Latin ebullientem (nominative ebulliens), present participle of ebullire to
boil over, literally and figuratively, from ex- out (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ex-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ex-</a>) + bullire to bubble
(see <a href=/index.php?term=boil&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>boil</a> (v.)).
Figurative sense of enthusiastic is first recorded 1660s.
