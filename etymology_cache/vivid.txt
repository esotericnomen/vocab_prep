1630s, from French vivide and perhaps also directly from Latin vividus spirited, animated, lively,
full of life, from vivus alive, from PIE *gweie- (1) to live (see <a
href=/index.php?term=bio-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>bio-</a>). Extension to colors
is from 1660s. Sense of strong, distinct (as of memories, etc.) is from 1680s; that of very active
or intense (as of imagination, interest, etc.) is from 1853. Related: Vividly; vividness.
