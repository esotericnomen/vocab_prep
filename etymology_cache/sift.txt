Old English siftan pass (something) through a sieve, from Proto-Germanic *sib- (cognates: Dutch
ziften, Middle Low German sichten, German sichten to sift; see <a
href=/index.php?term=sieve&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>sieve</a> (n.)). Intransitive
sense to pass loosely or fall scatteredly is from 1590s. Metaphoric sense of look carefully through
first recorded 1530s. Related: Sifted; sifting.
