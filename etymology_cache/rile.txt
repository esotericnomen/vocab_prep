1825, American English spelling alteration to reflect a dialectal pronunciation of <a
href=/index.php?term=roil&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>roil</a> (q.v.); compare <a
href=/index.php?term=heist&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>heist</a> from hoist and in
the same era spile for spoil (v.). Bartlett writes that in both England and America roil is now
commonly pronounced and written rile [Dictionary of Americanisms, 1848]. Related: Riled; riling.
