1560s, to cut with a gouge, from <a href=/index.php?term=gouge&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>gouge</a> (n.). Meaning to force out with a gouge (especially of the eyes, in
fighting) attested by 1800. Meaning swindle is American English colloquial from 1826 (implied in
plural noun gougers). Related: Gouged; gouging.
