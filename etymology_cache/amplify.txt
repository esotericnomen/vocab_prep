early 15c., to enlarge or expand, from Middle French amplifier, from Latin amplificare to enlarge,
from amplificus splendid, from amplus large (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ample&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ample</a>) + the root of
facere make, do (see <a href=/index.php?term=factitious&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>factitious</a>). Meaning augment in volume or amount is from 1570s. Restriction
of use to sound seems to have emerged in the electronic age, c.1915, in reference to radio
technology.
