1580s (earlier complice, late 15c.), from Old French complice a confederate, from Late Latin
complicem (nominative complex) partner, confederate, from Latin complicare fold together (see <a
href=/index.php?term=complicate&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>complicate</a>). With
parasitic a- on model of accomplish, etc., or perhaps by assimilation of indefinite article in
phrase a complice.
