early 14c., from Old French equite (13c.), from Latin aequitatem (nominative aequitas) equality,
conformity, symmetry, fairness, from aequus even, just, equal (see <a
href=/index.php?term=equal&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>equal</a> (adj.)). As the
name of a system of law, 1590s, from Roman naturalis aequitas, the general principles of justice
which corrected or supplemented the legal codes.
