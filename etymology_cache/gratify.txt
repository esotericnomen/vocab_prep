c.1400, to bestow grace upon; 1530s, to show gratitude to, from French gratifier (16c.) or directly
from Latin gratificari to do favor to, oblige, gratify, from gratus pleasing (see <a
href=/index.php?term=grace&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>grace</a> (n.)) + root of
facere make, do, perform (see <a href=/index.php?term=factitious&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>factitious</a>). Meaning to give pleasure to is from 1560s. Related: Gratified;
gratifying.
