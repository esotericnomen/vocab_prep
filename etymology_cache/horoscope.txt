c.1050, horoscopus, from Latin horoscopus; the modern form is considered to be a mid-16c.
reborrowing via Middle French horoscope. Ultimately from Greek horoskopos nativity, horoscope, also
one who casts a horoscope, from hora hour (see <a href=/index.php?term=year&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>year</a>) + skopos watcher; what is watched (see <a
href=/index.php?term=scope&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>scope</a> (n.1)), in
reference to the hour of one's birth.
