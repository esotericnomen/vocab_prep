late 14c., to kill, from Old French mortefiier destroy, overwhelm, punish, from Late Latin
mortificare cause death, kill, put to death, literally make dead, from mortificus producing death,
from Latin mors (genitive mortis) death (see <a href=/index.php?term=mortal&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>mortal</a> (adj.)) + root of facere to make (see <a
href=/index.php?term=factitious&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>factitious</a>).
Religious sense of to subdue the flesh by abstinence and discipline first attested early 15c. Sense
of humiliate first recorded 1690s (compare <a
href=/index.php?term=mortification&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>mortification</a>).
