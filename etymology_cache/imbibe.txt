late 14c., from Old French imbiber, embiber to soak into, from Latin imbibere absorb, drink in,
inhale, from assimilated form of in- into, in, on, upon (see <a
href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (2)) + bibere to drink,
related to potare to drink, from PIE *po(i)- to drink (see <a
href=/index.php?term=potion&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>potion</a>). Figurative
sense of mentally drink in (knowledge, ideas, etc.) was the main one in classical Latin, first
attested in English 1550s. Related: Imbibed; imbibing.
