Old English utlendisc of a foreign country, not native, from utland foreign land, literally outland
(see <a href=/index.php?term=out&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>out</a> + <a
href=/index.php?term=land&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>land</a> (n.)) + <a
href=/index.php?term=-ish&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>-ish</a>. Sense of unfamiliar,
strange, odd, bizarre (such as the customs of foreigners may seem to natives) is attested from
1590s.
