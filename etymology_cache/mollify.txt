late 14c., to soften (a substance), from Old French mollifier or directly from Late Latin
mollificare make soft, mollify from mollificus softening, from Latin mollis soft (see <a
href=/index.php?term=melt&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>melt</a> (v.)) + root of
facere to make (see <a href=/index.php?term=factitious&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>factitious</a>). Transferred sense of soften in temper, appease, pacify is
recorded from early 15c. Related: Mollified; mollifying.
