1660s, from Late Latin pandemus, from Greek pandemos pertaining to all people; public, common, from
pan- all (see <a href=/index.php?term=pan-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>pan-</a>) +
demos people (see <a href=/index.php?term=demotic&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>demotic</a>). Modeled on <a
href=/index.php?term=epidemic&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>epidemic</a>. The noun is
first recorded 1853, from the adjective.
