Old English wealwian to roll, from West Germanic *walwon, from PIE root *wel- (3) to roll (see <a
href=/index.php?term=volvox&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>volvox</a>). Figurative
sense of to plunge and remain in some state or condition is attested from early 13c. Related:
Wallowed; wallowing. The noun is recorded from 1590s as act of rolling; 1841 as place where an
animal wallows.
