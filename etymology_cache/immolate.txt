1540s, to sacrifice, kill as a victim, from Latin immolatus, past participle of immolare to
sacrifice, originally to sprinkle with sacrificial meal, from assimilated form of in- into, in, on,
upon (see <a href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (2)) +
mola (salsa) (sacrificial) meal, related to molere to grind (see <a
href=/index.php?term=mallet&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>mallet</a>). Related:
Immolated; immolating.
