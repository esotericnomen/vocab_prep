1530s, reversal of what is expected (especially a fatal turning point in a drama), from Latin
catastropha, from Greek katastrophe an overturning; a sudden end, from katastrephein to overturn,
turn down, trample on; to come to an end, from kata down (see <a
href=/index.php?term=cata-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>cata-</a>) + strephein turn
(see <a href=/index.php?term=strophe&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>strophe</a>).
Extension to sudden disaster is first recorded 1748.
