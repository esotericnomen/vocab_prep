late 14c., from Old French aparant evident, obvious, visible, from Latin apparentem (nominative
apparens) visible, manifest, present participle of apparere (see <a
href=/index.php?term=appear&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>appear</a>). First attested
in phrase heir apparent (see <a href=/index.php?term=heir&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>heir</a>). Meaning superficial is c.1400. Apparent magnitude in astronomy (how
bright a heavenly body looks from earth, as opposed to absolute magnitude, which is how bright it
really is) is attested from 1875.
