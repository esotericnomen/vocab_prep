early 15c., genealogical table or chart, from Anglo-French pe de gru, a variant of Old French pied
de gru foot of a crane, from Latin pedem accusative of pes foot (see <a
href=/index.php?term=foot&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>foot</a> (n.)) + gruem
(nominative grus) crane, cognate with Greek geranos, Old English cran; see <a
href=/index.php?term=crane&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>crane</a> (n.)).  <br /><br
/>  On old manuscripts, descent was indicated by a forked sign resembling the branching lines of a
genealogical chart; the sign also happened to look like a bird's footprint. Form influenced in
Middle English by association with degree. Meaning ancestral line is mid-15c.; of animals, c.1600.
Related: Pedigreed.
