1540s, from Middle French defraier (15c.), from de- out (see <a
href=/index.php?term=de-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>de-</a>) + fraier spend, from
Old French frais costs, damages caused by breakage, from Latin fractum, neuter past participle of
frangere to break (see <a href=/index.php?term=fraction&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>fraction</a>). Alternative etymology traces second element to Old High German
fridu peace, via Vulgar Latin *fredum fine, cost.
