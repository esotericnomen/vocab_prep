1530s, not capable of sin, from Middle French impeccable (15c.) or directly from Late Latin
impeccabilis not liable to sin, from assimilated form of in- not, opposite of (see <a
href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (1)) + pecare to sin,
of unknown origin. Meaning faultless is from 1610s. Related: Impeccably.
