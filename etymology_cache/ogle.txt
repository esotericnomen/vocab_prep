1680s, probably from Low German oeglen, frequentative of oegen look at, from oege eye, from Proto-
Germanic *augon-, from PIE *okw- to see (see <a href=/index.php?term=eye&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>eye</a> (n.)). Related to Dutch ogen to look at, from oog eye. Related: Ogled;
ogling. The noun meaning an amorous glance is attested from 1711; earlier it meant an eye (1700).
