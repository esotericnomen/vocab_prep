contradict, c.1300, literally say against, from Old English gegn- against (see <a
href=/index.php?term=again&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>again</a>) + <a
href=/index.php?term=say&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>say</a> (v.). Solitary survival
of a once common prefix [Weekley], which was used to form such now-obsolete compounds as gain-taking
taking back again, gainclap a counterstroke, gainbuy redeem, and gainstand to oppose. Related:
Gainsaid; gainsaying.
