mid-15c., to lower in worth or value, from Late Latin vilificare to make cheap or base; to esteem of
little value, from Latin vilis cheap, base (see <a href=/index.php?term=vile&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>vile</a>) + root of facere to make (see <a
href=/index.php?term=factitious&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>factitious</a>). Meaning
to slander, speak evil of is first recorded 1590s. Related: Vilified, vilifying.
