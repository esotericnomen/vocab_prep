late 12c., from Anglo-French curuner, from Latin custos placitorum coronae, originally the title of
the officer with the duty of protecting the property of the royal family, from Latin corona,
literally crown (see <a href=/index.php?term=crown&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>crown</a> (n.)). The duties of the office gradually narrowed and by 17c. the
chief function was to determine the cause of death in cases not obviously natural.
