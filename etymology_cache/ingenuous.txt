1590s, noble in nature, from Latin ingenuus with the virtues of freeborn people, of noble character,
frank, upright, candid, originally native, freeborn, from in- in (see <a
href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (2)) + gen-, root of
gignere beget, produce (see <a href=/index.php?term=genus&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>genus</a>). Sense of artless, innocent is 1670s, via evolution from high-minded
to honorably open, straightforward, to innocently frank. Related: Ingenuously; ingenuousness.
