early 14c., act of conceiving, from Old French concepcion (Modern French conception) conception,
grasp, comprehension, from Latin conceptionem (nominative conceptio) a comprehending, conception,
noun of action from stem of concipere (see <a href=/index.php?term=conceive&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>conceive</a>). Originally in the womb sense (also with reference to Conception
Day in the Church calendar); mental sense process of forming concepts is late 14c. Meaning that
which is conceived in the mind is from 1520s; general notion is from 1785.
