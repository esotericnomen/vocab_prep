1832, first used of a 15c.-16c. architectural style with flame-like curves, from French flamboyant
flaming, wavy, present participle of flamboyer to flame, from Old French flamboier (12c.), from
flambe flame, from flamble, variant of flamme, from Latin flammula (see <a
href=/index.php?term=flame&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>flame</a> (n.)). Extended
sense of showy, ornate is 1879. Related: Flamboyantly.
