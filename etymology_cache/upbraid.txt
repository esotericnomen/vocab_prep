Old English upbregdan bring forth as a ground for censure, from <a
href=/index.php?term=up&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>up</a> (adv.) + bregdan move
quickly, intertwine (see <a href=/index.php?term=braid&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>braid</a> (v.)). Similar formation in Middle Swedish upbrygdha. Meaning scold
is first attested late 13c. Related: Upbraided; upbraiding.
