1868, in reference to the raising of plants or animals, from Latin cultura tillage (see <a
href=/index.php?term=culture&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>culture</a>) + <a
href=/index.php?term=-al&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>-al</a> (1). In reference to
the cultivation of the mind, from 1875; hence, relating to civilization or a civilization. A fertile
starter-word among anthropologists and sociologists, for example cultural diffusion, in use by 1912;
cultural diversity by 1935; cultural imperialism by 1937; cultural pluralism by 1932; cultural
relativism by 1948.
