1690s, from French malfaisance wrongdoing, from malfaisant, from mal- badly (see <a
href=/index.php?term=mal-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>mal-</a>) + faisant, present
participle of faire to do, from Latin facere to do (see <a
href=/index.php?term=factitious&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>factitious</a>).
Malfeasor wrong-doer is attested from early 14c. Related: Malfeasant.
