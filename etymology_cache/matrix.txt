late 14c., uterus, womb, from Old French matrice womb, uterus, from Latin matrix (genitive matricis)
pregnant animal, in Late Latin womb, also source, origin, from mater (genitive matris) mother (see
<a href=/index.php?term=mother&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>mother</a> (n.1)). Sense
of place or medium where something is developed is first recorded 1550s; sense of embedding or
enclosing mass first recorded 1640s. Logical sense of array of possible combinations of truth-values
is attested from 1914. As a verb from 1951.
