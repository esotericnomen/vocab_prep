mid-12c., tresor, from Old French tresor treasury, hoard, treasure (11c., Modern French
tr&#233;sor), from Gallo-Roman *tresaurus, from Latin thesaurus treasury, treasure (source also of
Spanish, Italian tesoro), from Greek thesauros store, treasure, treasure house (see <a
href=/index.php?term=thesaurus&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>thesaurus</a>). In Middle
English also thresur, etc.; modern spelling is from 16c. Replaced Old English goldhord. General
sense of anything valued is recorded from c.1200. Treasure hunt is first recorded 1913. For treasure
trove, see <a href=/index.php?term=trove&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>trove</a>.
