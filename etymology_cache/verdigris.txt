c.1300, vertegrez, from Old French verte grez (13c.), verte de Grece (late 12c.), literally green of
Greece, from obsolete French verd, from Latin viridis (see <a
href=/index.php?term=verdure&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>verdure</a>). The reason
for it being called that is not known. In other languages, green of Spain (German gr&#252;nspan,
Danish spanskgr&#246;nt, Dutch spaansch-groen), from Medieval Latin viride Hispanum. Current
spelling in English is from 1789. In chemistry, confined to a basic copper acetate; popularly
applied to the green encrustation on copper or brass exposed to the air.
