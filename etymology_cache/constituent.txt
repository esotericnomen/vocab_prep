1620s, one who appoints or elects a representative, from Latin constituentem (nominative
constituens), present participle of constituere (see <a
href=/index.php?term=constitute&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>constitute</a>). The
notion is to make up or compose a body by appointing or electing a representative. As an adjective,
essential, characteristic, from 1660s; that appoints or elects a representative to a body, from
1714.
