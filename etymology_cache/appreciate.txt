1650s, to esteem or value highly, from Late Latin appretiatus, past participle of appretiare to set
a price to (see <a href=/index.php?term=appraise&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>appraise</a>). Meaning to rise in value (intransitive) first recorded 1789.
