1550s, ominous, from Middle French prodigieux and directly from Latin prodigiosus strange,
wonderful, marvelous, unnatural, from prodigium (see <a
href=/index.php?term=prodigy&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>prodigy</a>). Meaning vast,
enormous is from c.1600. Related: Prodigiously; prodigiosity.
