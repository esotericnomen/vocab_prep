c.1300, jaunis, from Old French jaunice, earlier jalnice, yellowness (12c.), from jaune yellow, from
Latin galbinus greenish yellow, probably from PIE *ghel- yellow, green (see <a

With intrusive -d- (compare gender, astound, thunder). Figurative meaning feeling in which views are
colored or distorted first recorded 1620s, from yellow's association with bitterness and envy (see
<a href=/index.php?term=yellow&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>yellow</a>). As a verb,
from 1791, but usually in figurative use. Related: Jaundiced.
