late 14c., foresight, prudent anticipation, from Old French providence divine providence, foresight
(12c.) and directly from Latin providentia foresight, precaution, foreknowledge, from providentem
(nominative providens), present participle of providere (see <a
href=/index.php?term=provide&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>provide</a>).  <br /><br />
Providence (usually capitalized) God as beneficent caretaker, first recorded c.1600, from earlier
use of the word for God's beneficient care or guidance (14c.), short for divine providence, etc. The
noun in Latin occasionally had a similar sense.
