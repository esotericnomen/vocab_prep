mid-15c., a curse, cursing, from Latin imprecationem (nominative imprecatio), from past participle
stem of imprecari invoke, pray, call down upon, from assimilated form of in- into, in, within (see
<a href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (2)) + precari to
pray, ask, beg, request (see <a href=/index.php?term=pray&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>pray</a>). Current limited sense is characteristic of human nature. [Weekley]
