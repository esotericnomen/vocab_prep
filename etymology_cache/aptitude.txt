early 15c., tendency, likelihood, from Middle French aptitude (14c.) or directly from Late Latin
aptitudo (genitive aptitudinis) fitness, noun of quality from Latin aptus joined, fitted (see <a
href=/index.php?term=apt&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>apt</a>). Meaning natural
capacity to learn is 1540s; that of quality of being fit (for a purpose or position) is from 1640s.
