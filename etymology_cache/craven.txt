early 13c., cravant, perhaps from Old French crevante defeated, past participle of cravanter to
strike down, to fall down, from Latin crepare to crack, creak. Sense affected by <a
href=/index.php?term=crave&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>crave</a> and moved from
defeated to cowardly (c.1400) perhaps via intermediary sense of confess oneself defeated. Related:
Cravenly; cravenness.
