1832, surroundings, environment, picked up by De Quincey from French entourage, from Middle French
entourer to surround (16c.), from Old French entour that which surrounds (10c.), from en- in (see <a
href=/index.php?term=en-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>en-</a> (1)) + tour a circuit
(see <a href=/index.php?term=tour&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>tour</a>). Sense of
attendant persons first recorded in English by 1860.
