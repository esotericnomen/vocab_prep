1820, from French doctrinaire impractical person, originally adherent of doctrines (14c.), from
Latin doctrina (see <a href=/index.php?term=doctrine&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>doctrine</a>).  <br /><br />  At first used in the context of French politics,
contemptuously applied by rival factions to those who tried to reconcile liberty with royal
authority after 1815. Hence, anyone who applies doctrine without making allowance for practical
considerations (1831). As an adjective, from 1834.
