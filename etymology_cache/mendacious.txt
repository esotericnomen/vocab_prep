1610s, from Middle French mendacieux, from Latin mendacium a lie, untruth, falsehood, fiction, from
mendax (genitive mendacis) lying, deceitful, from menda fault, defect, carelessness in writing, from
PIE root *mend- physical defect, fault (see <a href=/index.php?term=amend&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>amend</a> (v.)). The sense evolution of Latin mendax was influenced by mentiri
to speak falsely, lie, deceive. Related: Mendaciously; mendaciousness.
