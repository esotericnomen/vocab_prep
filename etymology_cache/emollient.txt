1640s, from French &eacute;mollient (16c.), from Latin emollientem (nominative emolliens), present
participle of emollire soften, from ex- out (see <a href=/index.php?term=ex-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>ex-</a>) + mollire soften, from mollis soft (see <a
href=/index.php?term=melt&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>melt</a> (v.)). The noun is
recorded from 1650s.
