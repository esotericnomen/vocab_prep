controvert (v.)

c.1600, probably a back-formation from controversy. Related: Controverted; controverting;
controvertible.


uncontroverted (adj.)

not liable to be called into question, 1640s, from un- (1) not + past participle of controvert (v.).


incontrovertible (adj.)

1640s, from in- (1) not + controvertible (see controvert). Related: Incontrovertibly.
