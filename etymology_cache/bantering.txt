roast (n.)

early 14c., meat roasted or for roasting; see roast (v.). Meaning a roasting is from 1580s. Sense of
an unmerciful bantering is from 1740.


banter (v.)

1670s, origin uncertain; said by Swift to be a word from London street slang. Related: Bantered;
bantering. The noun is from 1680s.
