1530s, to satisfy, satiate, from Latin saturatus, past participle of saturare to fill full, sate,
drench, from satur sated, full, from PIE root *sa- to satisfy (see <a
href=/index.php?term=sad&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>sad</a>). Meaning soak
thoroughly first recorded 1756. Marketing sense first recorded 1958. Related: Saturated; saturating.
