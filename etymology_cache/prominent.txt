mid-15c., projecting, jutting out, from Latin prominentem (nominative prominens) prominent, present
participle of prominere jut or stand out, be prominent, overhang, from pro- before, forward (see <a
href=/index.php?term=pro-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>pro-</a>) + minere to project,
from minae projections, threats (see <a href=/index.php?term=menace&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>menace</a> (n.)). Meaning conspicuous is from 1759; that of notable, leading is
from 1849. Related: Prominently.
