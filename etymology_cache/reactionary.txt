1831, on model of French r&eacute;actionnaire (19c.), from r&eacute;action (see <a
href=/index.php?term=reaction&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>reaction</a>). In Marxist
use, tending toward reversing existing tendencies, opposed to revolutionary and used opprobriously
in reference to opponents of communism, by 1858. As a noun, person considered reactionary,
especially in politics, one who seeks to check or undo political action, by 1855.
