conciseness (n.)

expression of much in few words, 1650s, from concise + -ness.  <blockquote>  [Conciseness] is the
English word familiar to the ordinary man: concision is the LITERARY CRITIC'S WORD, more recent in
English, used by writers under French influence &amp; often requiring the reader to stop &amp; think
whether he knows its meaning. [Fowler]  </blockquote>
