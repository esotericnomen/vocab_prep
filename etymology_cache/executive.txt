mid-15c., performed, carried out; 1640s, of the branch of government that carries out the laws, from
Middle French executif, from Latin executivus, from past participle stem of exequi (see <a
href=/index.php?term=execution&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>execution</a>). The noun
in this sense is from 1776, as a branch of government. Meaning businessman is 1902 in American
English. Executive privilege is attested by 1805, American English.
