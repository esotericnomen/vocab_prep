member of a vigilance committee, 1856, American English, from Spanish vigilante, literally watchman,
from Latin vigilantem (nominative vigilans) watchful, anxious, careful, from vigil (see <a
href=/index.php?term=vigil&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>vigil</a>). Vigilant man in
same sense is attested from 1824 in a Missouri context. Vigilance committees kept informal rough
order on the U.S. frontier or in other places where official authority was imperfect.
