1550s, from Latin transcribere to copy, write again in another place, write over, transfer, from
trans- over (see <a href=/index.php?term=trans-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>trans-</a>) + scribere write (see <a
href=/index.php?term=script&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>script</a> (n.)). To do it
poorly is to transcribble (1746). Related: Transcribed; transcriber; transcribing.
