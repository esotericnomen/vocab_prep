obtain something by trickery, 1888, originally British printer's slang for fake by manipulation;
perhaps an alteration of <a href=/index.php?term=waggle&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>waggle</a>, or of wankle (now dialectal) unsteady, fickle, from Old English
wancol (see <a href=/index.php?term=wench&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>wench</a>
(n.)). Brought into wider use by World War I soldiers.
