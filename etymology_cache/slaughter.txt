c.1300, killing of a cattle or sheep for food, killing of a person, from a Scandinavian *slahtr,
akin to Old Norse slatr a butchering, butcher meat, slatra to slaughter, slattr a mowing from Proto-
Germanic *slukhtis, related to Old Norse sla to strike (see <a
href=/index.php?term=slay&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>slay</a> (v.)) + formative
suffix (as in laugh/laughter). Meaning killing of a large number of persons in battle is attested
from mid-14c. Old English had slieht stroke, slaughter, murder, death; animals for slaughter; as in
sliehtswyn pig for killing.
