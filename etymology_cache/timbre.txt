percussive Middle Eastern instrument, c.1500, diminutive of timbre (14c.), from Old French timbre in
its older sense of drum (see <a href=/index.php?term=timbre&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>timbre</a>). Used in Bible translations, chiefly to render Hebrew toph, cognate
with Arabic duff drum, of imitative origin.
