1844, from Greek eskhatos last, furthest, uttermost, extreme, most remote (from ex out of, Boeotian
es-; see <a href=/index.php?term=ex-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ex-</a>) + <a
href=/index.php?term=-ology&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>-ology</a>. Originally in
theology, the study of the four last things: death, judgment, heaven, hell. Related: Eschatological;
eschatologically.
