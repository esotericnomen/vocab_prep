early 15c., proccy, prokecye, agency of one who acts instead of another; letter of power of
attorney, contraction of Anglo-French procuracie (c.1300), from Medieval Latin procuratia
administration, from Latin procuratio a caring for, management, from procurare manage (see <a
href=/index.php?term=procure&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>procure</a>). Also compare
<a href=/index.php?term=proctor&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>proctor</a> (n.).
Meaning person who acts in place of another is from 1610s.
