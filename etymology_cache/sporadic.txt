1680s, from Medieval Latin sporadicus scattered, from Greek sporadikos scattered, from sporas
(genitive sporados) scattered, dispersed, from spora a sowing (see <a
href=/index.php?term=spore&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>spore</a>). Originally a
medical term, occurring in scattered instances; the meaning happening at intervals is first recorded
1847. Related: Sporadical (1650s); sporadically.
