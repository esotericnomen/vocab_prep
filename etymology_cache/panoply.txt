1570s, from Greek panoplia complete suit of armor, from pan- all (see <a
href=/index.php?term=pan-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>pan-</a>) + hopla (plural),
arms of a hoplites (heavily armed soldier); see <a
href=/index.php?term=hoplite&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>hoplite</a>. Originally in
English figurative, of spiritual armor, etc. (a reference to Eph. vi); non-armorial sense of any
splendid array first recorded 1829.
