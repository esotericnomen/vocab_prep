c.1400, from Anglo-French alurer, Old French aleurer to attract, captivate; train a falcon to hunt,
from &agrave; to (see <a href=/index.php?term=ad-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>ad-</a>) + loirre falconer's lure, from a Frankish word (see <a
href=/index.php?term=lure&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>lure</a>), perhaps influenced
by French allure gait, way of walking. Related: Allured; <a
href=/index.php?term=alluring&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>alluring</a>. The noun is
first attested 1540s; properly this sense is <a
