1550s, from Middle French conflagration (16c.) or directly from Latin conflagrationem (nominative
conflagratio), present participle of conflagrare to burn up, from com-, intensive prefix (see <a
href=/index.php?term=com-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>com-</a>), + flagrare to burn
(see <a href=/index.php?term=flagrant&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>flagrant</a>).
