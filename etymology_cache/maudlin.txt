c.1600, tearful, from Middle English fem. proper name Maudelen (early 14c.), from Magdalene (Old
French Madelaine), woman's name, originally surname of Mary the repentant sinner forgiven by Jesus
in Luke vii:37 (see <a href=/index.php?term=Magdalene&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>Magdalene</a>). In paintings, she often was shown weeping as a sign of
repentance. Meaning characterized by tearful sentimentality is recorded by 1630s.
