Old English trog wooden vessel, tray, hollow vessel, canoe, from Proto-Germanic *trugaz (cognates:
Old Frisian, Old Saxon, Old Norse trog, Middle Dutch troch, Dutch trog, Old High German troc, German
trog), from PIE *dru-ko-, from root *dru-, *deru- wood, tree (see <a
href=/index.php?term=tree&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>tree</a> (n.)). Originally
pronounced in English with a hard -gh- (as in Scottish loch); pronunciation shifted to -ff, but
spelling remained.
