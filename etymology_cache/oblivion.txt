late 14c., state or fact of forgetting, from Old French oblivion (13c.) and directly from Latin
oblivionem (nominative oblivio) forgetfulness; a being forgotten, from oblivisci (past participle
oblitus) forget, originally even out, smooth over, efface, from ob over (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ob-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ob-</a>) + root of levis
smooth, from PIE *lei-w-, from root *(s)lei- slime, slimy, sticky (see <a
href=/index.php?term=slime&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>slime</a> (n.)). Meaning
state of being forgotten is early 15c.
