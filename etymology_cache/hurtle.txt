early 14c., hurteln, to crash together; to crash down, knock down, probably frequentative of hurten
(see <a href=/index.php?term=hurt&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>hurt</a> (v.)) in its
original sense. Intransitive meaning to rush, dash, charge is late 14c. The essential notion in
hurtle is that of forcible collision, in <a href=/index.php?term=hurl&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>hurl</a> that of forcible projection. Related: Hurtled; hurtling.
