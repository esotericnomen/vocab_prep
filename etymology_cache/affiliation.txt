1751, adoption, from French affiliation, from Medieval Latin affiliationem (nominative affiliatio),
noun of action from past participle stem of Latin affiliare to adopt a son, from ad- to (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ad-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ad-</a>) + filius son (see <a
href=/index.php?term=filial&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>filial</a>). Figurative
sense of adoption by a society, of branches first recorded 1799 (<a
href=/index.php?term=affiliate&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>affiliate</a> in this
sense is from 1761).
