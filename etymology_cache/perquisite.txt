mid-15c., property acquired other than by inheritance, from Medieval Latin perquisitum thing gained,
profit, in classical Latin, thing sought after, noun use of neuter past participle of perquirere to
seek, ask for, from per- thoroughly (see <a href=/index.php?term=per&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>per</a>) + qu&aelig;rere to seek (see <a
href=/index.php?term=query&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>query</a> (v.)). For Latin
vowel change, see <a href=/index.php?term=acquisition&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>acquisition</a>. General meaning fee or profit on top of regular wages first
recorded 1560s.
