early 14c., quiet, modest, demure, from Old French coi, earlier quei quiet, still, placid, gentle,
ultimately from Latin quietus resting, at rest (see <a
href=/index.php?term=quiet&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>quiet</a> (n.)). Meaning shy
emerged late 14c. Meaning unwilling to commit is 1961. Related: Coyly; coyness.
