late 14c., that to which one has recourse for aid or assistance, from Old French resort resource, a
help, an aid, a remedy, back-formation from resortir to resort, literally to go out again, from re-
again (see <a href=/index.php?term=re-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>re-</a>) + sortir
go out (see <a href=/index.php?term=sortie&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>sortie</a>).
Meaning place people go for recreation is first recorded 1754. Phrase in the last resort (1670s)
translates French en dernier ressort, originally of legal appeals.
