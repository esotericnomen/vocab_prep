late 15c. (implied in past participle expedit), from Latin expeditus, past participle of expedire
extricate, disengage, liberate; procure, make ready, make fit, prepare, literally free the feet from
fetters, hence liberate from difficulties, from ex- out (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ex-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ex-</a>) + *pedis fetter, chain
for the feet, related to pes (genitive pedis) foot (see <a
href=/index.php?term=foot&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>foot</a> (n.)). Compare Greek
pede fetter. Related: Expedited; expediting.
