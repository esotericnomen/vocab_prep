1580s, from Middle French bataillon (16c.), from Italian battaglione battle squadron, from
diminutive of Vulgar Latin battalia battle, from Latin bauttere to beat (see <a
href=/index.php?term=batter&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>batter</a> (v.)). Specific
sense of part of a regiment is from 1708.<blockquote>Madame, lui r&eacute;pondit-il, ne vous y fiez
pas: j'ay t&ocirc;ujours v&#251; Dieu do cot&eacute; des gros Batallions. [E.Boursault,
1702]</blockquote>
