1610s, from Latin acclivitatem (nominative acclivitas) an ascending direction, an upward steepness,
from acclivis mounting upwards, ascending, from ad- up (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ad-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ad-</a>) + clivus hill, a
slope, from PIE *klei-wo-, suffixed form of *klei- to lean (see <a
href=/index.php?term=lean&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>lean</a> (v.)).
