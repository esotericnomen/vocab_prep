late 15c., sign, portent, something extraordinary from which omens are drawn, from Latin prodigium
prophetic sign, omen, portent, prodigy, from pro- forth (see <a
href=/index.php?term=pro-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>pro-</a>) + -igium, a suffix
or word of unknown origin, perhaps from *agi-, root of aio I say (see <a
href=/index.php?term=adage&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>adage</a>). Meaning child
with exceptional abilities first recorded 1650s.
