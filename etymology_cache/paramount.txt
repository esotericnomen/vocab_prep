1530s, from Anglo-French paramont, Old French paramont above (in place, order, degree), mid-14c.,
from Old French par by, from Latin per through, for, by (see <a
href=/index.php?term=per&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>per</a> (prep.)) + amont up,
from a mont upward (see <a href=/index.php?term=amount&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>amount</a> (v.)). The whole from Latin per ad montem, literally to the hill.
