1630s, from French parez! (which commonly would have been heard in fencing lessons), imperative of
parer ward off, from Italian parare to ward or defend a blow (see <a
href=/index.php?term=para-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>para-</a> (2)). Related:
Parried; parrying. Non-fencing use is from 1718. The noun is 1705, from the verb.
