mid-15c., plot where plants are raised from seeds, from Latin seminarium plant nursery, seed plot,
figuratively, breeding ground, from seminarius of seed, from semen (genitive seminis) seed (see <a
href=/index.php?term=semen&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>semen</a>). Meaning school
for training priests first recorded 1580s; commonly used for any school (especially academies for
young ladies) from 1580s to 1930s.
