remediable (adj.)

1560s, from Middle French rem&#233;diable, from Latin remediabilis that may be healed, curable, from
stem of remediare, from remedium (see remedy (n.)).


irremediable (adj.)

mid-15c., from Late Latin irremediabilis, from assimilated form of in- not, opposite of (see in-
(1)) + remediabilis (see remediable).
