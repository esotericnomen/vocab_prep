1550s, favored food or drink of the gods, from Latin ambrosia, from Greek ambrosia food of the gods,
fem. of ambrosios, probably literally of the immortals, from a- not (see <a
href=/index.php?term=a-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>a-</a> (3)) + mbrotos, related
to mortos mortal, from PIE *mer- to die (see <a href=/index.php?term=mortal&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>mortal</a> (adj.)). Applied to certain herbs by Pliny and Dioscorides; used of
various foods for mortals since 1680s (originally of fruit drinks); used figuratively for anything
delightful by 1731.
