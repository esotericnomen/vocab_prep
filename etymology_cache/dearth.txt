mid-13c., derthe scarcity (originally used of famines, when food was costly because scarce; extended
to other situations of scarcity from early 14c.), abstract noun formed from root of Old English
deore precious, costly (see <a href=/index.php?term=dear&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>dear</a>) + abstract noun suffix <a
href=/index.php?term=-th&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>-th</a> (2). Common Germanic
formation, though not always with the same sense (cognates: Old Saxon diurtha splendor, glory, love,
Middle Dutch dierte, Dutch duurte, Old High German tiurida glory).
