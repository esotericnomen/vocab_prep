c.1300, value, worth, from Old French valor, valour valor, moral worth, merit, courage, virtue
(12c.), from Late Latin valorem (nominative valor) value, worth (in Medieval Latin strength, valor),
from stem of Latin valere be strong, be worth (see <a
href=/index.php?term=valiant&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>valiant</a>). The meaning
courage is first recorded 1580s, from Italian valore, from the same Late Latin word. (The Middle
English word also had a sense of worth or worthiness in respect of manly qualities).
