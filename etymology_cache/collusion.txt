late 14c., from Old French collusion, from Latin collusionem (nominative collusio) act of colluding,
from colludere, from com- together (see <a href=/index.php?term=com-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>com-</a>) + ludere to play, from ludus game (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ludicrous&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ludicrous</a>). The
notion of fraud or underhandedness is essential to collusion [Fowler].
