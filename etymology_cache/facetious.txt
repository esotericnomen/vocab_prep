1590s, from French fac&eacute;tieux, from fac&eacute;tie a joke (15c.), from Latin facetia jest,
witticism, from facetus witty, elegant, fine, courteous, of unknown origin, perhaps related to facis
torch.  <br /><br />  It implies a desire to be amusing, often intrusive or ill-timed.  Related:
Facetiously; facetiousness. Faceti&aelig; in booksellers' catalogues, is, like curious, a euphemism
for erotica. [Fowler]
