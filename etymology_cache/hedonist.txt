1822, in reference to the Cyrenaic school of philosophy that deals with the ethics of pleasure, from
Greek hedonikos pleasurable, from hedone pleasure, related to hedys sweet, cognate with Latin suavis
(see <a href=/index.php?term=sweet&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>sweet</a>). A
hedonist is properly the follower of any ethical system in which some sort of pleasure ranks as the
highest good. The Epicurian identifies this pleasure with the practice of virtue.
