1620s, from Latin discriminatus, past participle of discriminare to divide, separate, from discrimen
(genitive discriminis) interval, distinction, difference, derived noun from discernere (see <a
href=/index.php?term=discern&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>discern</a>). The adverse
(usually racial) sense is first recorded 1866, American English. Positive sense remains in <a
href=/index.php?term=discriminating&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>discriminating</a>.
Related: Discriminated. Also used 17c. and after as an adjective meaning distinct.
