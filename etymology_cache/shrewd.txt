c.1300, wicked, evil, from shrewe wicked man (see <a
href=/index.php?term=shrew&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>shrew</a>). Compare crabbed
from <a href=/index.php?term=crab&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>crab</a> (n.), dogged
from <a href=/index.php?term=dog&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>dog</a> (n.), wicked
from <a href=/index.php?term=witch&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>witch</a> (n.). The
sense of cunning is first recorded 1510s. Related: Shrewdly; shrewdness. Strutt's Sports and
Pastimes of the People of England (1801) has a shrewdness of apes for a company or group of them.
Shrewdie cunning person is from 1916.
