early 15c., supreme happiness, from Middle French b&eacute;atitude (15c.) and directly from Latin
beatitudinem (nominative beatitudo) state of blessedness, from past participle stem of beare make
happy (see <a href=/index.php?term=bene-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>bene-</a>). As
a declaration of blessedness (usually plural, beatitudes, especially in reference to the Sermon on
the Mount) it is attested from 1520s.
