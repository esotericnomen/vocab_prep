degradation (n.)

1530s, from French d&eacute;gradation (14c., Old French degradacion), from Medieval Latin
degradationem (nominative degradatio), noun of action from past participle stem of degradare (see
degrade).


aetio-

word-forming element used in chemistry and indicating a fundamental degradation product of a complex
organic compound [Flood], from Latinized comb. form of Greek aitia a cause, an origin (see
etiology).


villainy (n.)

c.1200, from Anglo-French vilanie, Old French vilenie low character, unworthy act, disgrace,
degradation, from vilain (see villain).
