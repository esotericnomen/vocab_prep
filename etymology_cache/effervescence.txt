1650s, the action of boiling up, from French effervescence (1640s), from Latin effervescentem,
present participle of effervescere to boil up, boil over, from ex- out (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ex-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ex-</a>) + fervescere begin to
boil, from fervere be hot, boil (see <a href=/index.php?term=brew&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>brew</a>). Figurative sense of liveliness is from 1748. Related: Effervescency.
