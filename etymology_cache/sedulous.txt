1530s, from Latin sedulus attentive, painstaking, diligent, busy, zealous, probably from sedulo
(adv.) sincerely, diligently, from sedolo without deception or guile, from se- without, apart (see
<a href=/index.php?term=secret&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>secret</a>) + dolo,
ablative of dolus deception, guile, cognate with Greek dolos ruse, snare. Related: Sedulously;
sedulousness.
