late 14c., wanting in self restraint, from Old French incontinent, from Latin incontinentem
(nominative incontinens) incontinent, immoderate, intemperate, from in- not, opposite of (see <a
href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (1)) + continens (see
<a href=/index.php?term=continent&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>continent</a>).
Originally chiefly of sexual appetites; sense of unable to control bowels or bladder first attested
1828.
