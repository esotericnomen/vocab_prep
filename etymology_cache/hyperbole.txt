early 15c., from Latin hyperbole, from Greek hyperbole exaggeration, extravagance, related to
hyperballein to throw over or beyond, from hyper- beyond + bole a throwing, a casting, the stroke of
a missile, bolt, beam, from bol-, nominative stem of ballein to throw (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ballistics&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ballistics</a>).
Rhetorical sense is found in Aristotle and Isocrates.
