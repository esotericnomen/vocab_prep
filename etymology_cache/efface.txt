late 15c., from Middle French effacer, from Old French esfacier (12c.) to wipe out, destroy,
literally to remove the face, from es- out (see <a href=/index.php?term=ex-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>ex-</a>) + face appearance, from Latin facies face (see <a
href=/index.php?term=face&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>face</a> (n.)). Related:
Effaced; effacing. Compare <a href=/index.php?term=deface&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>deface</a>.
