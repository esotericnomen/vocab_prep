late 14c., of the air, made of air, from <a href=/index.php?term=air&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>air</a> (n.1) + <a href=/index.php?term=-y&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>-y</a> (2). Meaning breezy is attested from 1590s; that of lively is from
1640s. Sense of vain, unsubstantial is from 1580s. Disparaging airy-fairy is attested from 1920
(earlier in a sense of delicate or light as a fairy, which is how Tennyson used it in 1830).
