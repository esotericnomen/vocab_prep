1540s, justle, to knock against, formed from jousten (see <a
href=/index.php?term=joust&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>joust</a>) + frequentative
suffix -tle. The usual spelling 17c.-18c. was justle. An earlier meaning of the word was to have sex
with (c.1400). Meaning to contend for the best position or place is from 1610s. Related: Jostled;
jostling. As a noun from c.1600.
