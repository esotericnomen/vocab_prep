mid-14c., from Old French chevaleros knightly, noble, chivalrous, from chevalier (see <a
href=/index.php?term=chevalier&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>chevalier</a>; also
compare <a href=/index.php?term=chivalry&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>chivalry</a>).
According to OED, obsolete in English and French from mid-16c. Not revived in French, but brought
back in English late 18c. by romantic writers fond of medieval settings.
