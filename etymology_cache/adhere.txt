1590s, from Middle French adh&eacute;rer (15c.) or directly from Latin adhaerare to stick to (see <a
href=/index.php?term=adherent&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>adherent</a> (adj.)).
Originally often of persons, to cleave to a leader, cause, party, etc. (compare adherent (n.), which
still often retains this sense). Related: Adhered; adhering.
