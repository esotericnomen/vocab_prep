1550s, from Medieval Latin incineratus reduced to ashes, pp. of incinerare, from Latin in- into (see
<a href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (2)) + cinis
(genitive cineris) ashes, from PIE root *keni- dust, ashes (cognates: Greek konis dust). Used
earlier in English as a past participle adjective meaning reduced to ashes (early 15c.). Related:
Incinerated; incinerating.
