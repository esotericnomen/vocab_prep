Old English glowan to glow, shine as if red-hot, from Proto-Germanic base *glo- (cognates: Old Saxon
gloian, Old Frisian gled glow, blaze, Old Norse gloa, Old High German gluoen, German gl&uuml;hen to
glow), from PIE *ghel- (2) to shine, with derivatives referring to bright materials and gold (see <a
href=/index.php?term=glass&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>glass</a>). Figuratively from
late 14c. Related: Glowed; glowing.
