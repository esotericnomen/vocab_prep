Old English uncu&eth; unknown, strange, unusual; uncertain, unfamiliar; unfriendly, unkind, rough,
from <a href=/index.php?term=un-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>un-</a> (1) not +
cu&eth; known, well-known, past participle of cunnan to know (see <a
href=/index.php?term=can&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>can</a> (v.1)). Meaning
strange, crude, clumsy is first recorded 1510s. The compound (and the thing it describes) widespread
in IE languages, such as Latin ignorantem, Old Norse uku&eth;r, Gothic unkun&thorn;s, Sanskrit
ajnatah, Armenian ancanaut', Greek agnotos, Old Irish ingnad unknown.
