1620s, knowledge of law, from French jurisprudence (17c.) and directly from Late Latin
iurisprudentia the science of law, from iuris of right, of law (genitive of ius; see <a
href=/index.php?term=jurist&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>jurist</a>) + prudentia
knowledge, a foreseeing (see <a href=/index.php?term=prudence&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>prudence</a>). Meaning the philosophy of law is first attested 1756. Related:
Jurisprudential.
