early 15c., assertion that something is true, from Old French afermacion (14c.), from Latin
affirmationem (nominative affirmatio) an affirmation, solid assurance, noun of action from past
participle stem of affirmare (see <a href=/index.php?term=affirm&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>affirm</a>). In law, as the Quaker alternative to oath-taking, it is attested
from 1690s.
