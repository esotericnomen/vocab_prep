mid-14c., minister of a chapel, from Old French chapelein clergyman (Modern French chapelain), from
Medieval Latin cappellanus clergyman, originally custodian of St. Martin's cloak (see <a
href=/index.php?term=chapel&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>chapel</a>). Replaced Old
English capellane (from the same Medieval Latin source) clergyman who conducts private religious
services, originally in great households, later in military regiments, prisons, etc.
