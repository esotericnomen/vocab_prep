early 14c., from Old French benigne (12c., kind, benign, merciful, gracious; Modern French
b&eacute;nin, fem. b&eacute;nigne), from Latin benignus kindly, kindhearted, friendly, generous,
literally well born, from bene well (see <a href=/index.php?term=bene-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>bene-</a>) + gignere to bear, beget, from genus birth (see <a
href=/index.php?term=genus&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>genus</a>). For similar sense
evolution, compare <a href=/index.php?term=gentle&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>gentle</a>, <a href=/index.php?term=kind&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>kind</a> (adj.), <a href=/index.php?term=generous&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>generous</a>. Related: Benignly.
