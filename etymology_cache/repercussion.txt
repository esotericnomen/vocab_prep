early 15c., act of driving back, from Middle French r&eacute;percussion (14c.) or directly from
Latin repercusionem (nominative repercussio), from past participle stem of repercutere to strike or
beat back; shine back, reflect; echo, from re- back (see <a
href=/index.php?term=re-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>re-</a>) + percutere to strike
or thrust through (see <a href=/index.php?term=percussion&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>percussion</a>). Meaning reverberation, echo first recorded 1590s; the
metaphoric extension is recorded from 1620s.
