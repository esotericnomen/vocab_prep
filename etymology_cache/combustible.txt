1520s, from Middle French combustible, or directly from Late Latin combustibilis, from Latin
combustus, past participle of combuere to burn up, consume (see <a
href=/index.php?term=combustion&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>combustion</a>).
Figurative sense is from 1640s; as a noun, from 1680s. Related: Combustibility (late 15c.).
