mid-15c., from Latin aspersionem (nominative aspersio) a sprinkling, noun of action from past
participle stem of aspergere to sprinkle on, from ad- to (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ad-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ad-</a>) + spargere sprinkle,
strew (see <a href=/index.php?term=sparse&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>sparse</a>).
Originally in theology, the shedding of Christ's blood. Modern sense of a bespattering with slander
first attested 1590s. To cast aspersions was in Fielding (1749).
