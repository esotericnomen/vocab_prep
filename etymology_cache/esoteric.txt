1650s, from Greek esoterikos belonging to an inner circle, from esotero more within, comparative
adverb of eso within, related to eis into, en in (see <a
href=/index.php?term=en-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>en-</a> (2)).  <br /><br />  In
English, originally of Pythagorean doctrines. According to Lucian, the division of teachings into
exoteric and esoteric originated with Aristotle.
