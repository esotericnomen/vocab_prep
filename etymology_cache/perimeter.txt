early 15c., line around a figure or surface, from Latin perimetros, from Greek perimetron
circumference, from peri-  around (see <a href=/index.php?term=peri-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>peri-</a>) + metron measure (see <a
href=/index.php?term=meter&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>meter</a> (n.2)). Military
sense of boundary of a defended position is attested from 1943.
