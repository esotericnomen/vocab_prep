late 14c., born prematurely or dead, from Latin abortivus pertaining to miscarriage; causing
abortion, from abort-, past participle stem of aboriri disappear, miscarry, from ab- amiss (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ab-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ab-</a>) + oriri appear, be
born, arise (see <a href=/index.php?term=orchestra&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>orchestra</a>); the compound word used in Latin for deaths, miscarriages,
sunsets, etc. The Latin verb for to produce an abortion was abigo, literally to drive away. Not
originally used to imply forced or deliberate miscarriage; from 14c.-18c. stillborn children or
domestic animals were said to be abortive. Also see <a
href=/index.php?term=abortion&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>abortion</a>. Related:
Abortiveness.
