1590s, pertaining to the hypochondria, also afflicted with melancholy, from French hypocondriaque
(16c.), from Medieval Latin hypochondriacus, from Greek hypokhondriakos pertaining to the upper
abdomen, from hypokhondria (see <a href=/index.php?term=hypochondria&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>hypochondria</a>). The noun is from 1630s, melancholy person; in the modern
sense from 1888.
