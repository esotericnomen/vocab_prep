mid-15c., barren, from Middle French st&eacute;rile not producing fruit, from Latin sterilis barren,
unproductive, unfruitful; unrequited; unprofitable, from PIE *ster- (1) stiff, rigid, firm, strong
(see <a href=/index.php?term=stereo-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>stereo-</a>). Also
see <a href=/index.php?term=torpor&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>torpor</a>.
Originally in English with reference to soil; of persons (chiefly females), from 1530s. The sense of
sterilized, free from living germs is first recorded 1877.
