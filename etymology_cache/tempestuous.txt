late 14c., from Late Latin tempestuosus stormy, turbulent, from Latin tempestas storm, commotion,
time (see <a href=/index.php?term=tempest&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>tempest</a>).
The figurative sense is older in English; literal sense is from c.1500. Related: Tempestuously;
tempestuousness.
