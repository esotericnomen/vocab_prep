early 15c., from Latin dissipatus, past participle of dissipare to spread abroad, scatter, disperse;
squander, disintegrate, from dis- apart (see <a href=/index.php?term=dis-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>dis-</a>) + supare to throw, scatter, from PIE *swep- to throw, sling, cast
(cognates: Lithuanian supu to swing, rock, Old Church Slavonic supo to strew). Related: Dissipated;
dissipates; dissipating.
