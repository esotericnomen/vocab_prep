1823, from French r&eacute;calcitrant, literally kicking back (17c.-18c.), past participle of
recalcitrare to kick back; be inaccessible, from re- back (see <a
href=/index.php?term=re-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>re-</a>) + Latin calcitrare to
kick, from calx (genitive calcis) heel. Used from 1797 as a French word in English.
