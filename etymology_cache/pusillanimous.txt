early 15c., from Late Latin pusillanimis having little courage (used in Church Latin to translate
Greek oligopsychos small-souled), from Latin pusillis very weak, little (diminutive of pullus young
animal; see <a href=/index.php?term=foal&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>foal</a> (n.))
+ animus spirit, courage (see <a href=/index.php?term=animus&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>animus</a>). Related: Pusillanimously; pusillanimousness.
