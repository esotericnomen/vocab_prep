plagiarize (v.)

1716, from plagiary plagiarist (see plagiarism) + -ize. Related: Plagiarized; plagiarizing.


plagiarise (v.)

alternative (chiefly British) spelling of plagiarize. Related: Plagiarised; plagiarising.


crib (v.)

steal, 17c. from crib (n.) in a secondary sense a basket; this probably also is the source of
student slang meaning plagiarize (1778). Related: Cribbed; cribbing.


surreptitious (adj.)

mid-15c., from Latin surrepticius stolen, furtive, clandestine, from surreptus, past participle of
surripere seize secretly, take away, steal, plagiarize, from assimilated form of sub from under
(hence, secretly; see sub-) + rapere to snatch (see rapid). Related: Surreptitiously.
