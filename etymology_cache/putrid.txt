early 15c., from Latin putridus, from putrere to rot, from putris rotten, crumbling, related to
putere to stink, from PIE root *pu- to rot, stink (see <a
href=/index.php?term=pus&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>pus</a>). First in reference to
putrid fever, an old name for typhus (also known in Middle English as putrida). Related:
Putrification.
