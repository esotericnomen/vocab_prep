1640s, from French liaison a union, a binding together (13c.), from Late Latin ligationem
(nominative ligatio) a binding, from past participle stem of Latin ligare to bind (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ligament&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ligament</a>). Originally
a cookery term for a thickening agent for sauces. Sense of intimate relations is from 1806. Military
sense of cooperation between branches, allies, etc. is from 1816. The noun meaning one who is
concerned with liaison of units, etc. is short for liaison officer.
