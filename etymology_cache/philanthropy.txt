c.1600, from Late Latin philanthropia, from Greek philanthropia kindliness, humanity, benevolence,
love to mankind (from gods, men, or things), from philanthropos (adj.) loving mankind, useful to
man, from phil- loving  (see <a href=/index.php?term=philo-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>philo-</a>) + anthropos mankind (see <a
href=/index.php?term=anthropo-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>anthropo-</a>).
Originally in English in the Late Latin form; modern spelling attested from 1620s.
