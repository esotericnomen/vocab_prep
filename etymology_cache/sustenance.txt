c.1300, means of living, subsistence, livelihood, from Old French sostenance support, aid (Modern
French soutenance), from Late Latin sustinentia endurance, from present participle stem of Latin
sustinere (see <a href=/index.php?term=sustain&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>sustain</a>). Meaning action of sustaining life by food is from late 14c. Sense
of nourishment is recorded from late 15c. Related: Sustenant.
