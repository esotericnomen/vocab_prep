late 14c., unaccented syllable or note, from Latin thesis unaccented syllable in poetry, later (and
more correctly) stressed part of a metrical foot, from Greek thesis a proposition, also downbeat (in
music), originally a setting down, a placing, an arranging; position, situation, from root of
tithenai to place, put, set, from PIE root *dhe- to set, to put (see <a
href=/index.php?term=factitious&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>factitious</a>). Sense
in logic of a formulation in advance of a proposition to be proved is first recorded 1570s; that of
dissertation presented by a candidate for a university degree is from 1650s.
