1799, to accuse, bring charges against, from Medieval Latin inculpatus, past participle of inculpare
to reproach, blame, censure, from Latin in- in (see <a
href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (2)) + culpare to
blame, from culpa fault. But inculpable (late 15c.) means not culpable, free from blame, from Latin
in- not (see <a href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>in-</a> (1)) +
culpare.
