1560s, from Greek hegemonia leadership, a leading the way, a going first; also the authority or
sovereignty of one city-state over a number of others, as Athens in Attica, Thebes in Boeotia; from
hegemon leader, from hegeisthai to lead, perhaps originally to track down, from PIE *sag-eyo-, from
root *sag- to seek out, track down, trace (see <a href=/index.php?term=seek&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>seek</a>). Originally of predominance of one city state or another in Greek
history; in reference to modern situations from 1860, at first of Prussia in relation to other
German states.
