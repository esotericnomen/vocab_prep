1640s, from Medieval Latin homogeneus, from Greek homogenes of the same kind, from homos same (see
<a href=/index.php?term=homo-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>homo-</a> (1)) + genos
kind, gender, race, stock (see <a href=/index.php?term=genus&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>genus</a>). Earlier in this sense was homogeneal (c.1600).
