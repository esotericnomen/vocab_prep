late 14c., a going around; a line going around, from Old French circuit (14c.) a circuit; a journey
(around something), from Latin circuitus a going around, from stem of circuire, circumire go around,
from circum round (see <a href=/index.php?term=circum-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>circum-</a>) + ire to go (see <a
href=/index.php?term=ion&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ion</a>). Electrical sense is
from 1746. Of judicial assignments, from 1570s; of venues for itinerant entertainers, from 1834.
Circuit breaker is recorded from 1874. Related: Circuital.
