1590s, out of the way, from Latin devius out of the way, remote, off the main road, from de via (see
<a href=/index.php?term=deviate&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>deviate</a>). Originally
in the Latin literal sense; figurative sense of deceitful is first recorded 1630s. Related:
Deviously; deviousness. Figurative senses of the Latin word were retired, sequestered, wandering in
the byways, foolish, inconsistent.
