1570s, from Latin obsoletus grown old, worn out, past participle of obsolescere fall into disuse,
probably from ob away (see <a href=/index.php?term=ob-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>ob-</a>) + an expanded form of solere to be used to, be accustomed (see <a
href=/index.php?term=insolent&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>insolent</a>).
