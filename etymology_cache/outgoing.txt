1630s, that goes out, from <a href=/index.php?term=out&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>out</a> (adv.) + <a href=/index.php?term=going&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>going</a>. Meaning sociable, friendly, attested from 1950, on same notion as in
<a href=/index.php?term=extrovert&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>extrovert</a>. Middle
English had a noun outgoing a departure, mid-14c., from a verb outgo to go forth, and Old English
had utgangende outgoing (literal). Related: Outgoingness.
