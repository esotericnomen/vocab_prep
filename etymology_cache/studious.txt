mid-14c. (implied in studiously) zealous, diligent, eager, from Latin studiosus devoted to study,
assiduous, zealous, from studium eagerness, zeal (see <a
href=/index.php?term=study&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>study</a>). From late 14c. as
eager to learn, devoted to learning, also, as noun, those who study or read diligently. Related:
Studiousness.
