mid-14c., from Anglo-French conspiracie, Old French conspiracie conspiracy, plot, from Latin
conspirationem (nominative conspiratio) agreement, union, unanimity, noun of action from conspirare
(see <a href=/index.php?term=conspire&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>conspire</a>);
earlier in same sense was conspiration (early 14c.), from French conspiration (13c.), from Latin
conspirationem. An Old English word for it was facengecwis. As a term in law, from 1863. Conspiracy
theory is from 1909.
