1550s, from Middle French inexorable and directly from Latin inexorabilis that cannot be moved by
entreaty, from in- not, opposite of (see <a href=/index.php?term=in-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>in-</a> (1)) + exorabilis able to be entreated, from exorare to prevail upon,
from ex- out (see <a href=/index.php?term=ex-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ex-</a>) +
orare pray (see <a href=/index.php?term=orator&amp;allowed_in_frame=0
class=crossreference>orator</a>). Related: Inexorably; inexorability.
