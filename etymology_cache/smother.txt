c.1200, to suffocate with smoke, from smother (n.), earlier smorthre dense, suffocating smoke (late
12c.), from stem of Old English smorian to suffocate, choke, strangle, stifle, cognate with Middle
Dutch smoren, German schmoren; possibly connected to <a
href=/index.php?term=smolder&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>smolder</a>. Meaning to
kill by suffocation in any manner is from 1540s; sense of to extinguish a fire is from 1590s. Sense
of stifle, repress is first recorded 1570s; meaning to cover thickly (with some substance) is from
1590s. Related: Smothered; smothering.
