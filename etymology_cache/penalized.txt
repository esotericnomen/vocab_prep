clipping (n.1)

early 13c., clasping, embracing, verbal noun from clip (v.2). As a U.S. football penalty (not in
OED), from 1920.  <blockquote> Clipping or Cutting Down from Behind. -- This is to be ruled under
unnecessary roughness, and penalized when it is practiced upon a man obviously out of the play. This
clipping is a tendency in the game that the committee is watching anxiously and with some fear.
[Colliers, April 10, 1920]  </blockquote>
