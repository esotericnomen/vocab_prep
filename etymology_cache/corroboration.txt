corroboration (n.)

mid-15c., strengthening, support, from Late Latin corroborationem (nominative corroboratio), noun of
action from past participle stem of Latin corroborare to strengthen (see corroborate). Meaning
confirmation attested by 1768.


verification (n.)

1520s, from Medieval Latin *verificationem (nominative verificatio), noun of action from past
participle stem of verificare (see verify). Middle English had verifiaunce confirmation,
corroboration (c. 1400).
