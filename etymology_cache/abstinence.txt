mid-14c., forbearance in indulgence of the appetites, from Old French abstinence (earlier
astenance), from Latin abstinentia abstinence, starvation; self-restraint, integrity, noun of
quality from abstinentem (nominative abstinens), present participle of abstinere (see <a
href=/index.php?term=abstain&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>abstain</a>). Specifically
of sexual appetites from mid-14c., but also in Middle English of food, fighting, luxury.
