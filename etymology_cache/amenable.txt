1590s, liable, from Anglo-French amenable, Middle French amener answerable (to the law), from
&agrave; to (see <a href=/index.php?term=ad-&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>ad-</a>) +
mener to lead, from Latin minare to drive (cattle) with shouts, variant of minari threaten (see <a
href=/index.php?term=menace&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>menace</a> (n.)). Sense of
tractable is from 1803, from notion of disposed to answer or submit to influence. Related: Amenably.
