1570s, haughty, imperious, alteration of Middle English sirly lordly, imperious (14c.), literally
like a sir, from <a href=/index.php?term=sir&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>sir</a> +
<a href=/index.php?term=-ly&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>-ly</a> (1). The meaning
rude, gruff is first attested 1660s. For sense development, compare <a
href=/index.php?term=lordly&amp;allowed_in_frame=0 class=crossreference>lordly</a>, and German
herrisch domineering, imperious, from Herr master, lord. Related: Surliness.
