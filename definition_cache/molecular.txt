Use the adjective molecular to describe something that has to do with or is made up of molecules. If
you're studying molecular biology, you're focusing on the molecules that make up living things.

When a scientist studies things on a molecular level, she's looking at them up close, examining
their cells. The adjective molecular comes up most often in biology and chemistry, and it always
describes the very smallest units that make up organisms or elements. Molecular comes from the noun
molecule, which means extremely minute particle, from the Latin molecula, tiny mass.
