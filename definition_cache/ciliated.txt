Something that’s ciliated is covered in microscopic projections that look like tiny hairs.
Ciliated cells use a sweeping motion to remove toxins from your lungs.

Ciliated is pronounced sih-lee-ay-TED. This adjective describes something that has tiny hair-like
projections called cilia. Ciliated might be a new word to your vocabulary, but your body knows all
about it — ciliated cells are found throughout it. Your lungs, for example, are ciliated.
