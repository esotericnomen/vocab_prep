As a noun, reserve refers to the quality of a shy or modest person who doesn’t easily express his
or her feelings. As a verb, to reserve is to stash something away or to set it aside for future use.

Reserve can also refer to backup supplies or resources. If a military is running out of ammunition
or food, they may have to dig into the reserve to replenish their supplies. Reserve is also used as
a verb to mean obtain in advance. If you’re expecting a big crowd at your sister's dance recital,
you might show up early to reserve seats for your family members. You could also reserve a table at
a restaurant for dinner after the show.
