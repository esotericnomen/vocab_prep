If you see your mother every day, but seldom see your dad because he works late, you might be
grateful for the opportunity to hang with him on the weekends. Use the adjective seldom to refer to
things that don't happen very often.

A near synonym is rarely. Seldom is a Middle English word, from Old English seldum, a spelling
alteration of seldan strange, rare. In Old English, the spelling seldum came about by analogy with
forms such as Old English hwilum formerly, at one time, from hwil time, while.
