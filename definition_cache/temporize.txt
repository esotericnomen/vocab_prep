The verb temporize describes stalling in order to gain time in the hopes of avoiding or preventing
something. Your class might temporize so your teacher won't have time to give the pop quiz you all
think she's going to give.

Accent the first syllable of temporize: TEM-puh-rise. Tempor- means time, which you probably
recognize from other time-related words such as temporary, temporal, and contemporary. Temporize
comes from the Middle French word temporiser, meaning to pass one's time, wait one's time.
