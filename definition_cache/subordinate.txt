Subordinate means putting one thing below another––a subordinate is someone who works for
someone else, and to subordinate means to place or rank one thing below another. We subordinate our
desire for popcorn to our desire to keep watching the movie.

The ordinate part of subordinate refers to an ordering of things, and sub- means lower. In the army,
a private is subordinate to an officer, whether that officer is his direct commander or not. When
it's a noun or adjective, the word is pronounced suh-BOR-duh-nit, and when it's a verb, suh-BOR-duh-
nate.
