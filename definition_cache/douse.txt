Use the verb douse to describe covering something with water or other liquid. When you're camping,
you douse the campfire with water when you're done with it.

Douse often involves water, but you could also douse your French fries in ketchup. Douse can
describe the act of extinguishing a candle, or even turning off a light. You might douse your
bedroom light when it's time to sleep or simply douse a candle by blowing it out. To pronounce douse
correctly, remember that it rhymes with mouse.
